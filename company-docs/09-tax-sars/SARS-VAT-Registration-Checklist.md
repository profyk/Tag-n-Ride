## DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR VAT101 APPLICATION

### 1. Company Documents
- [ ] Certified copy of CK1/COR14.3 (CIPC registration certificate)
- [ ] Certified copy of COR15.1A (Memorandum of Incorporation)
- [ ] CIPC company extract (not older than 3 months)

### 2. Director / Representative Vendor
- [ ] Certified copy of SA ID document (green barcoded ID or Smart ID card)
- [ ] SARS income tax reference number (the representative vendor)
- [ ] Proof of address (municipality account or bank statement, not older than 3 months)

### 3. Banking Details
- [ ] Original letter from bank confirming account details (bank name, account number, branch code, account type)
- [ ] Account must be in the company's registered name

### 4. Business Details
- [ ] Proof of business address (lease agreement or utility bill)
- [ ] Description of main business activity (ISIC code for fintech/software)
- [ ] Financial projection or first invoices confirming turnover

### 5. Tax Compliance
- [ ] Company income tax registration confirmation (CIT reference number)
- [ ] PAYE registration (if applicable)
- [ ] No outstanding returns or debt with SARS (confirm via eFiling)

## SUBMISSION PROCESS

1. Register or log in to **SARS eFiling** (www.sarsefiling.co.za)
2. Navigate to **Register New > VAT**
3. Complete **VAT101** form online
4. Upload certified supporting documents
5. SARS will conduct a business address verification visit within 21 days
6. VAT registration certificate (VAT103) issued once approved

**Estimated Processing Time:** 10–21 business days

## VAT INVOICE REQUIREMENTS (s20 VAT Act)

All invoices issued by Tag-n-Ride must contain:
- The words "TAX INVOICE"
- Company name and VAT registration number
- Invoice number (sequential)
- Date of invoice
- Buyer's name and address (and VAT number if B2B ≥ R5,000)
- Description of goods/services
- Quantity and price
- VAT amount shown separately
- Total including VAT

---

## IMPORTANT NOTES

- **Platform fees / commissions** earned from drivers are taxable supplies at 15% VAT
- **Wallet top-ups** are financial services — may qualify as **exempt** supplies (seek legal advice)
- **FICA-gated transactions** — VAT treatment depends on whether service or financial instrument
- Input tax claims: only on **valid tax invoices** received from VAT-registered suppliers
