# PROVISIONAL TAX (IRP6) GUIDE
## TAG-N-RIDE (PTY) LTD

**Ref:** TNR-TAX-PROV-001
**Applicable Law:** Income Tax Act 58 of 1962, s84–s89A
**SARS Definition:** Provisional tax is a method of paying income tax in advance to avoid a large lump-sum at year-end.

---

## WHO MUST PAY

Tag-n-Ride (Pty) Ltd is a **juristic person** and therefore a **mandatory provisional taxpayer**.

All companies must submit and pay provisional tax regardless of profitability.

---

## CALCULATING THE ESTIMATE

### Basic Method
```
Estimated Taxable Income = Gross Revenue - Deductible Expenses - Depreciation - Losses
```

### Tax Calculation (Companies — 2025/2026)
| Entity | Tax Rate |
|--------|---------|
| Companies (normal) | 27% flat |
| Small Business Corporations (s12E) | Progressive: 0% / 7% / 21% / 27% |
| Dividends Withholding Tax | 20% |

### Penalty Threshold (2nd Provisional)
If your 2nd provisional estimate is **less than 80%** of actual taxable income → SARS imposes a **20% underestimation penalty** on the shortfall.

## PAYMENT

- Pay via SARS eFiling (EFT or credit push)
- SARS issues a payment notice (PRN — Payment Reference Number)
- Keep proof of payment for 5 years
- Interest (repo rate + 3.5%) applies on late payments from due date

---

## AUDIT TRIGGER CONSIDERATIONS

SARS may scrutinize if:
- Declared taxable income differs significantly from prior years without explanation
- VAT turnover does not reconcile with income tax return
- Directors' loans (s7C tax) are not reported
- No tax was paid despite visible revenue on bank statements

## SMALL BUSINESS CORPORATION (s12E) TEST

Tag-n-Ride may qualify if:
- Gross income ≤ **R20,000,000** per year
- All shareholders are natural persons
- Shareholders hold no shares in any other company
- No personal service income ≥ 20% of gross income

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| R0 – R95,750 | 0% |
| R95,751 – R365,000 | 7% on amount above R95,750 |
| R365,001 – R550,000 | 21% on amount above R365,000 |
| Above R550,000 | 27% |

**Note:** As Tag-n-Ride takes on institutional investors, the natural-person shareholder requirement may be lost. Review annually with tax advisor.
