## 3. COMMITTEE PURPOSE AND MANDATE

The **[COMMITTEE NAME]** has been established by the Board with the following mandate:

*[Select and customise the applicable committee description:]*

### Audit Committee
The Audit Committee is established in terms of section 94 of the Companies Act 71 of 2008 and is responsible for:

(a) Reviewing the integrity and reliability of the financial statements;

(b) Overseeing the internal and external audit functions;

(c) Assessing the effectiveness of internal controls, risk management, and compliance systems;

(d) Approving the appointment, fees, and terms of engagement of the external auditor;

(e) Ensuring financial reporting complies with the Companies Act and IFRS / SA GAAP;

(f) Reviewing and approving the annual report.

### Remuneration Committee
The Remuneration Committee is responsible for:

(a) Reviewing and recommending to the Board the remuneration policy for executive directors and senior management;

(b) Setting performance targets and recommending bonuses and incentive schemes;

(c) Ensuring that remuneration practices support the Company's strategy and are aligned with BBBEE and employment equity objectives;

(d) Reviewing and recommending non-executive directors' fees for shareholder approval.

### Risk Committee
The Risk Committee is responsible for:

(a) Overseeing the Company's enterprise risk management framework;

(b) Reviewing and monitoring key risks, including financial, operational, regulatory, cyber, and reputational risks;

(c) Reviewing the Company's insurance arrangements;

(d) Ensuring compliance with all applicable legal and regulatory risk requirements, including the Financial Sector Regulation Act 9 of 2017 (where applicable);

(e) Reporting on material risks to the Board.

### Social and Ethics Committee
The Social and Ethics Committee is established in terms of section 72(4) of the Companies Act 71 of 2008 and Regulation 43 of the Companies Regulations, 2011, and is responsible for:

(a) Monitoring the Company's compliance with relevant legislation and best practices in the areas of social and economic development, BBBEE, employment equity, and environmental protection;

(b) Reporting to shareholders at the Annual General Meeting;

(c) Reviewing the Company's ethical conduct, including its Code of Conduct and anti-corruption policies.

## 9. CONFIDENTIALITY

9.1 You are required to maintain strict confidentiality in respect of:

(a) All matters discussed and information reviewed at Committee meetings;

(b) All documentation provided to the Committee;

(c) All resolutions and recommendations of the Committee before they are formally published.

9.2 Confidentiality obligations apply during your term and for a period of **three (3) years** after your appointment ends.

9.3 Deliberations of the Committee are privileged and may not be disclosed to third parties without the Company's written consent, except as required by law.

---

## 10. INDEPENDENCE AND CONFLICTS

10.1 Where your appointment is as an **independent** Committee member, you must:

(a) Satisfy the independence criteria applicable to the relevant committee under the Companies Act and King IV;

(b) Notify the Chairman of the Board immediately if any circumstance arises that may affect your independence;

(c) Disclose all personal financial interests in matters before the Committee.

10.2 Audit Committee members must satisfy the requirements of section 94(4) of the Companies Act, which provides that Audit Committee members may not be:

(a) Involved in the day-to-day management of the Company;

(b) Prescribed officers of the Company;

(c) Directors who are employees of the Company or its subsidiaries (except where the Company has no independent non-executive directors).

---

## 11. RESIGNATION AND REMOVAL

11.1 You may resign from the Committee on thirty (30) days' written notice to the Chairman.

11.2 The Board may remove you from the Committee by resolution at any time, for cause or by ordinary resolution.

11.3 You shall automatically cease to be a Committee member if your directorship or employment with the Company terminates.
