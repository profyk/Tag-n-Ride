## 4. FIDUCIARY DUTIES

By accepting this appointment, you acknowledge and undertake to comply with the following duties imposed by the **Companies Act 71 of 2008**:

### 4.1 Section 76 — Standards of Directors' Conduct

Under section 76 of the Companies Act, you must:

4.1.1 Act in good faith and in the best interests of the Company;

4.1.2 Act with the degree of care, skill, and diligence that may reasonably be expected of a person carrying out the same functions in relation to a company as those carried out by you, and having the general knowledge, skill, and experience of you;

4.1.3 Act within your powers and with authority conferred by the MOI and the Companies Act;

4.1.4 Disclose any personal financial interests in matters to be decided by the Board, in accordance with section 75 of the Companies Act, and recuse yourself from voting on such matters where required;

4.1.5 Not use your position as director, or information obtained as a result of your position, to gain any advantage for yourself or any other person that is to the detriment of the Company.

### 4.2 Section 77 — Liability of Directors

Under section 77 of the Companies Act, you acknowledge that you may be held personally liable for loss, damages, or costs sustained by the Company as a result of:

4.2.1 Any breach of a fiduciary duty under section 76;

4.2.2 Acting recklessly or with gross negligence;

4.2.3 Acting in a manner that knowingly causes harm to the Company;

4.2.4 Being a party to an act or omission by the Company constituting an offence under the Companies Act or any other legislation.

### 4.3 Section 75 — Personal Financial Interests

You are required to disclose any personal financial interest in any matter to be considered by the Board, in accordance with section 75 of the Companies Act, before the matter is considered.
