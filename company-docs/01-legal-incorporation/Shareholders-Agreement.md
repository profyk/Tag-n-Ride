## 3. GOVERNANCE AND MANAGEMENT

### 3.1 Board Composition

3.1.1 The Board shall initially comprise **Mr Profy T D Keakile** as sole director.

3.1.2 Upon any investor acquiring **15% or more** of Shares, such investor shall have the right to appoint one (1) non-executive director to the Board.

3.1.3 Upon any investor acquiring **30% or more** of Shares, such investor shall have the right to appoint two (2) non-executive directors to the Board.

3.1.4 The Founder shall at all times retain the right to appoint the majority of the Board until an Exit Event or unless the Founder holds fewer than **20%** of Shares.

### 3.2 CEO Authority

3.2.1 Mr Profy T D Keakile, as CEO, shall have executive authority to manage the day-to-day operations of the Company.

3.2.2 The CEO may not take the following actions without Board approval:
  (a) Commit the Company to expenditure exceeding **R100,000** in any single transaction;
  (b) Hire or dismiss senior management (VP level and above);
  (c) Enter into any partnership, licensing, or joint venture agreement;
  (d) Incur any debt or financial obligation exceeding **R200,000**.

## 4. SHAREHOLDER RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS

### 4.1 Information Rights

Each Shareholder holding **5% or more** of Shares shall be entitled to receive:
  (a) Monthly management accounts within **15 days** of month-end;
  (b) Audited annual financial statements within **90 days** of financial year-end;
  (c) Quarterly business updates and key performance metrics.

### 4.2 Anti-Dilution

4.2.1 Shareholders holding **5% or more** shall have a **pro-rata pre-emptive right** to participate in any new share issuance to maintain their percentage shareholding, except for:
  (a) ESOP shares;
  (b) Shares issued in an Exit Event approved by 75% of shareholders.

4.2.2 Pre-emptive rights must be exercised within **15 (fifteen) business days** of written notice of a new issuance.

### 4.3 Dividend Policy

4.3.1 Dividends shall be declared at the discretion of the Board, subject to the Company meeting its solvency and liquidity requirements under the Act.

4.3.2 During the growth phase, the Company's policy is to reinvest profits. Dividends will only be declared once the Company achieves sustained profitability and the Board deems it appropriate.

## 5. TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

### 5.1 Lock-Up Period

No Shareholder may transfer any Shares during the **36 (thirty-six) month** period following the date of this Agreement, without the written consent of Shareholders holding at least 75% of the Shares ("**Lock-Up Period**").

### 5.2 Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

5.2.1 After the Lock-Up Period, a Shareholder wishing to transfer Shares ("**Selling Shareholder**") must first offer them to the remaining Shareholders.

5.2.2 The Selling Shareholder shall give written notice ("**Transfer Notice**") specifying: the number of Shares, the proposed price, and the identity of the proposed transferee.

5.2.3 Existing Shareholders shall have **20 (twenty) business days** to exercise their ROFR by written notice.

5.2.4 If the ROFR is not fully exercised, the Company shall have a further **10 (ten) business days** to purchase the remaining Shares.

5.2.5 If neither existing Shareholders nor the Company exercise the ROFR in full, the Selling Shareholder may transfer to the proposed transferee on terms no better than those specified in the Transfer Notice.

### 5.3 Tag-Along Rights

If any Shareholder proposes to sell **25% or more** of the total Shares to a third party, all other Shareholders shall have the right to participate in such sale on the same terms and at the same price, pro rata to their shareholding.

### 5.4 Drag-Along Rights

If Shareholders holding **75% or more** of all Shares accept a bona fide offer from a third party to acquire **all** Shares, they may require all remaining Shareholders to sell their Shares to such third party on the same terms.

## 7. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

### 7.1 Non-Compete

During the period of shareholding and for **24 (twenty-four) months** after ceasing to be a Shareholder, each Shareholder shall not:
  (a) Directly or indirectly engage in, own, manage, or invest in a business that is in direct competition with the Company in the Republic of South Africa;
  (b) Use the Company's Intellectual Property, trade secrets, or confidential information for any competing purpose.

### 7.2 Non-Solicitation

During the period of shareholding and for **24 (twenty-four) months** after ceasing to be a Shareholder, each Shareholder shall not:
  (a) Solicit or induce any employee of the Company to leave employment;
  (b) Solicit any client, customer, or partner of the Company for a competing business.

### 7.3 Reasonableness

The Parties acknowledge that these restrictions are reasonable, given the nature of the fintech industry and the Company's legitimate business interests.

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 Each Shareholder shall keep confidential all proprietary and commercially sensitive information of the Company.

8.2 This obligation survives the termination of this Agreement for **5 (five) years**.

8.3 Confidential information may only be disclosed:
  (a) To professional advisors under a duty of confidentiality;
  (b) As required by law or a court order;
  (c) With the prior written consent of the Board.

---

## 9. DEADLOCK

9.1 If the Shareholders are unable to resolve a material dispute affecting the governance or operation of the Company ("Deadlock"), either party may invoke the Deadlock procedure.

9.2 Upon a Deadlock, the Parties shall first attempt resolution through senior management engagement within **15 days**.

9.3 If unresolved, the matter shall be referred to an independent mediator agreed upon by the Parties, or appointed by the AFSA.

9.4 If mediation fails within **30 days**, either party may invoke **arbitration** as per the MOI's dispute resolution clause.

---

## 10. EXIT PROVISIONS

### 10.1 IPO

The Parties shall co-operate in good faith to facilitate an IPO if the Board determines this to be in the best interests of the Company, subject to approval by Shareholders holding 75% of Shares.

### 10.2 Trade Sale

Any sale of all or substantially all the business or assets of the Company shall require approval by Shareholders holding 75% of Shares.

### 10.3 Founder's Right to Lead Exit

The Founder (Mr Profy T D Keakile) shall have the right to lead any Exit Event negotiations on behalf of all Shareholders, provided all Shareholders are kept informed and any binding terms are subject to Shareholder approval as set out above.
