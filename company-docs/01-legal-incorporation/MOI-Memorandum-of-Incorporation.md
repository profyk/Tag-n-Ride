## PART B — SHARES AND SHAREHOLDERS

### 4. AUTHORISED SHARE CAPITAL

4.1 The Company is authorised to issue **10,000,000 (Ten Million)** ordinary shares with no par value ("Shares").

4.2 Shares may be issued in such classes as the Board may determine, including but not limited to:

| Class | Description | Votes per Share |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Class A — Ordinary | Founder and standard investor shares | 1 vote |
| Class B — Preference | Investor preference shares (if issued) | Non-voting unless specified |
| Class C — ESOP | Employee Share Option Pool shares | Non-voting until exercised |

4.3 The Board may, by resolution, create additional classes of shares, subject to shareholder approval as required by the Act.

---

### 5. EMPLOYEE SHARE OPTION POOL (ESOP)

5.1 A pool of **1,000,000 (One Million)** Shares, representing **10% (ten percent)** of the authorised share capital, is hereby reserved for issuance under the Company's Employee Share Option Plan ("ESOP") as detailed in the ESOP Plan document.

5.2 No ESOP shares shall be issued without prior Board approval.

5.3 Unvested or unexercised ESOP shares that are forfeited shall return to the ESOP pool.

---

### 6. TRANSFER OF SHARES

6.1 Shares in the Company are not freely transferable. Any proposed transfer of Shares shall be subject to:

  (a) **Right of First Refusal (ROFR):** Existing shareholders shall have a right of first refusal to purchase the Shares proposed to be transferred, on the same terms offered by any bona fide third party, in proportion to their existing shareholding.

  (b) **Board Approval:** No transfer of Shares shall be registered unless approved in writing by the Board.

  (c) **Tag-Along Rights:** If a shareholder holding 50% or more of Shares proposes to sell to a third party, minority shareholders shall have the right to sell their Shares to such third party on the same terms.

  (d) **Drag-Along Rights:** If shareholders holding 75% or more of Shares approve a sale of the Company, they may require remaining shareholders to sell on the same terms.

6.2 The above restrictions shall not apply to transfers to wholly-owned subsidiaries or family trusts of the transferring shareholder, subject to Board approval.

---

### 7. SHAREHOLDERS' MEETINGS

7.1 Annual General Meetings ("AGM") shall be held within **6 (six) months** after the end of each financial year.

7.2 Extraordinary General Meetings ("EGM") may be called by:
  (a) The Board at any time; or
  (b) Shareholders holding at least **10%** of voting rights, by written request to the Board.

7.3 Notice of at least **15 (fifteen) business days** shall be given for all shareholder meetings.

7.4 **Quorum:** A quorum for shareholder meetings shall be shareholders holding at least **51%** of voting rights, present in person or by proxy.

7.5 **Resolutions:**
  (a) Ordinary resolutions require a simple majority (more than 50%) of votes cast.
  (b) Special resolutions require at least 75% of votes cast.

---

## PART C — DIRECTORS AND MANAGEMENT

### 9. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

9.1 The minimum number of directors is **1 (one)** and the maximum is **7 (seven)**.

9.2 The initial director is **Mr Profy T D Keakile**, who shall serve as Executive Director, CEO, and CTO.

9.3 Directors shall be appointed by ordinary resolution of shareholders, except that the Board may appoint directors to fill a vacancy, subject to confirmation at the next general meeting.

9.4 **Removal of Directors:** A director may be removed by ordinary resolution of shareholders, provided that the director has been given reasonable notice and the opportunity to make representations.

---

### 10. POWERS OF THE BOARD

10.1 The Board has full authority to manage and run the Company's business, subject to:
  (a) This MOI;
  (b) The Act;
  (c) Any shareholder resolutions.

10.2 The Board may delegate any of its powers to:
  (a) Executive management;
  (b) Committees of the Board; or
  (c) Any other person, subject to retaining ultimate accountability.

10.3 **Reserved Matters requiring Board approval:**
  (a) Annual budget and business plan;
  (b) Capital expenditure exceeding **R50,000** per transaction;
  (c) Entering into contracts with a value exceeding **R100,000**;
  (d) Hiring or dismissing C-suite employees;
  (e) Issuance of ESOP grants;
  (f) Entering new markets or launching new product lines;
  (g) Incurring debt or financial obligations exceeding **R200,000**.

---

### 11. BOARD MEETINGS

11.1 Board meetings shall be held at least **quarterly**.

11.2 Notice of at least **5 (five) business days** shall be given for Board meetings, except in urgent circumstances.

11.3 **Quorum:** A majority of directors then in office, with a minimum of **2 (two)** directors once the Board has more than one member.

11.4 Resolutions of the Board require a simple majority of votes cast. In the event of a tie, the Chairperson shall have a casting vote.

11.5 Meetings may be held via video conference or telephone, provided all participants can communicate with each other.

11.6 **Round Robin Resolutions:** A decision supported in writing by all directors is as valid as a resolution passed at a meeting.

---

### 12. FINANCIAL YEAR AND AUDIT

12.1 The Company's financial year end shall be **28/29 February** of each year, unless changed by Board resolution.

12.2 The Company shall maintain proper financial records and prepare annual financial statements in accordance with the Act and applicable accounting standards.

12.3 An independent review or audit shall be conducted as required by the Act.
