# EMPLOYEE SHARE OPTION PLAN (ESOP)
## TAG-N-RIDE (PTY) LTD

**Adopted by the Board of Directors on:** [DATE]
**Administered by:** Board of Directors

---

## 1. PURPOSE

1.1 The purpose of this Employee Share Option Plan ("**Plan**") is to:

  (a) Attract and retain talented employees, contractors, and advisors;
  (b) Align the interests of team members with the long-term success of Tag-n-Ride (Pty) Ltd ("the Company");
  (c) Reward performance and commitment;
  (d) Enable participation in the Company's growth and value creation.

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **"Administrator"** means the Board of Directors, or any committee delegated by the Board.
- **"Exercise Price"** means the price per Share at which an Option may be exercised, as determined by the Board at the date of Grant.
- **"Good Leaver"** means a Participant who departs due to death, permanent disability, retrenchment, or with the written consent of the Board.
- **"Bad Leaver"** means any departure that is not a Good Leaver event.
- **"Grant Date"** means the date on which an Option is formally awarded to a Participant.
- **"Option"** means the right (but not the obligation) to acquire Shares in the Company at the Exercise Price.
- **"Option Agreement"** means the individual agreement between the Company and a Participant setting out the specific terms of an Option grant.
- **"Participant"** means any eligible employee, contractor, or advisor who receives an Option grant.
- **"Pool"** means the ESOP pool of **1,000,000 (One Million)** Shares, representing **10%** of the authorised share capital, reserved for this Plan.
- **"Shares"** means Class C ESOP ordinary shares in the Company.
- **"Vest"** means the process by which Options become exercisable over time according to the Vesting Schedule.
- **"Vesting Schedule"** means the schedule set out in Clause 6.

---

## 3. SHARE POOL

3.1 The total number of Shares available under this Plan shall not exceed **1,000,000 (One Million) Shares**, representing **10% (ten percent)** of the Company's fully diluted share capital.

3.2 Shares that are forfeited, cancelled, or not exercised shall return to the Pool and become available for future grants.

3.3 Any increase to the Pool requires a Board resolution and shareholder approval by Special Resolution.

---

## 4. ELIGIBILITY

4.1 The following persons are eligible to receive Option grants:
  (a) Full-time and part-time employees of the Company;
  (b) Contractors and consultants engaged on a long-term basis (minimum 6 months);
  (c) Advisors approved by the Board.

4.2 Eligibility does not guarantee a grant. All grants are at the Board's sole discretion.

---

## 5. GRANT OF OPTIONS

### 5.1 Grant Process

5.1.1 The Board shall approve all Option grants by resolution.

5.1.2 Each grant shall be evidenced by a written **Option Grant Letter** signed by the Company and the Participant.

5.1.3 Option grants shall specify:
  (a) Number of Options granted;
  (b) Exercise Price;
  (c) Grant Date;
  (d) Vesting Schedule;
  (e) Expiry Date.

### 5.2 Exercise Price

5.2.1 The Exercise Price shall be set by the Board at the Grant Date, typically at:
  (a) **Fair market value** of a Share at the Grant Date; or
  (b) Such other price as the Board may determine, including nominal value for key early hires.

5.2.2 The Board shall document the basis for fair market value determination.

---

## 6. VESTING

### 6.1 Standard Vesting Schedule

Unless otherwise specified in the Option Grant Letter, Options shall vest as follows:

| Period | Options Vesting | Cumulative |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| Months 0–12 (Cliff) | 0% | 0% |
| Month 12 (Cliff unlock) | 25% | 25% |
| Months 13–48 (monthly) | 1/48th per month | Up to 100% |

*Total vesting: 4 years with a 1-year cliff.*

### 6.2 Accelerated Vesting

6.2.1 **Exit Event:** Upon an Exit Event (trade sale, merger, acquisition, or IPO), all unvested Options shall vest immediately (single-trigger acceleration), unless the Board determines otherwise in the interest of the transaction.

6.2.2 **Good Leaver:** Upon a Good Leaver event, all unvested Options vest immediately, and the Participant (or their estate) shall have **90 (ninety) days** to exercise.

### 6.3 Forfeiture

6.3.1 Upon a **Bad Leaver** event, all unvested Options are forfeited immediately and returned to the Pool.

6.3.2 Vested but unexercised Options upon a Bad Leaver event shall remain exercisable for **30 (thirty) days** after departure, after which they expire.

---

## 7. EXERCISE OF OPTIONS

### 7.1 Exercise Notice

To exercise vested Options, the Participant must submit a written Exercise Notice to the Company specifying the number of Options to be exercised.

### 7.2 Payment

Payment of the Exercise Price must accompany the Exercise Notice by:
  (a) Cash or EFT;
  (b) Such other mechanism as the Board may approve (e.g., cashless exercise at an Exit Event).

### 7.3 Issuance of Shares

Upon receipt of the Exercise Notice and payment, the Company shall issue the relevant Shares to the Participant and update the share register within **10 (ten) business days**.

---

## 8. EXPIRY OF OPTIONS

8.1 Options shall expire on the earliest of:
  (a) **10 (ten) years** from the Grant Date;
  (b) **90 days** after a Good Leaver event;
  (c) **30 days** after a Bad Leaver event;
  (d) Immediately upon termination for gross misconduct or fraud.

---

## 9. RESTRICTIONS ON ESOP SHARES

9.1 ESOP Shares are **non-voting** until exercised.

9.2 Upon exercise, ESOP Shares convert to ordinary Class A Shares and carry one vote per Share.

9.3 ESOP Shares are subject to the same transfer restrictions as other Shares under the Shareholders Agreement, including ROFR and Lock-Up provisions.

9.4 Participants may not pledge, encumber, or transfer unexercised Options.

---

## 10. DILUTION AND ANTI-DILUTION

10.1 The ESOP Pool shall be maintained on a **fully diluted basis**, meaning the Pool is calculated as a percentage of all issued shares plus the total reserved Pool.

10.2 In the event of a share split, consolidation, or bonus issue, the number of Options and Exercise Price shall be adjusted proportionally.

---

## 11. TAX

11.1 Participants are responsible for their own tax obligations arising from the grant, vesting, or exercise of Options.

11.2 The Company shall deduct PAYE from any deemed income arising from Option exercises as required under the Income Tax Act 58 of 1962 and SARS guidance on equity instruments.

11.3 The Company shall issue relevant tax certificates to assist Participants with their tax filings.

---

## 12. AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION OF THE PLAN

12.1 The Board may amend this Plan at any time by resolution, provided that no amendment shall materially and adversely affect the rights of any existing Participant without their written consent.

12.2 The Plan shall terminate upon:
  (a) A Board resolution to terminate, provided all outstanding Options are honoured; or
  (b) An Exit Event, unless the acquirer assumes the Plan obligations.

---

## 13. ADMINISTRATION

13.1 The Board shall maintain a register of all Option grants, vesting status, and exercises.

13.2 Participants shall receive an annual statement of their Options, vesting status, and current indicative value.

13.3 All decisions of the Board regarding this Plan shall be final and binding, unless challenged through the dispute resolution mechanism in the MOI.

---

## 14. GOVERNING LAW

This Plan is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa.
