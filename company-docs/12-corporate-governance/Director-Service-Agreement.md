## 1. APPOINTMENT

1.1 The Company appoints the Director as **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** and as an **Executive Director** of the Board, and the Director accepts this appointment on the terms set out in this Agreement.

1.2 The appointment is effective from **[START DATE]** and continues until terminated in accordance with clause 10.

1.3 The Director acknowledges the duties and responsibilities of a director as set out in ss76–77 of the Companies Act, including:
- Duty to act in good faith and in the best interests of the Company
- Duty to act with the care, skill, and diligence of a reasonably diligent person
- Duty to avoid conflicts of interest
- Duty not to use Company information for personal gain

The Director shall:

2.1 Lead and manage the day-to-day operations of the Company as CEO.

2.2 Execute the strategic plan and business goals as approved by the Board.

2.3 Manage the executive team and ensure delivery against KPIs.

2.4 Represent the Company with investors, regulators, customers, and media.

2.5 Ensure the Company complies with all applicable laws and regulations, including the Companies Act, POPIA, FICA, and relevant fintech regulations.

2.6 Ensure timely SARS submissions, CIPC annual returns, and statutory filings.

2.7 Prepare and present monthly management accounts and board reports.

2.8 Not make capital expenditure or commitments in excess of **R[AMOUNT]** without Board approval.

2.9 Devote **substantially all** working time and attention to the Company's business.

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 All IP created by the Director in the course of employment (including platform code, business plans, algorithms, customer lists, and any inventions) belongs exclusively to the Company.

5.2 The Director hereby assigns (and will execute further instruments of assignment as required) all such IP to the Company.

---

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 The Director shall not disclose any Confidential Information of the Company to any third party during or after employment, unless required by law.

6.2 "Confidential Information" includes: business plans, financials, investor details, source code, customer data, regulatory strategies, and any information not in the public domain.

6.3 This obligation survives termination for **5 years** (or indefinitely for trade secrets).

---

## 7. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

7.1 During the term of this Agreement and for **24 months** after termination, the Director shall not:

(a) **Non-compete:** Directly or indirectly own, manage, operate, or be employed by any business that competes with the Company in the South African fintech/ride payment market — except as agreed in writing by the Board.

(b) **Non-solicitation (staff):** Induce or attempt to induce any employee of the Company to leave employment.

(c) **Non-solicitation (customers):** Solicit, canvass, or deal with any customer, partner, or investor of the Company for competitive purposes.

7.2 The Director acknowledges that these restrictions are reasonable given access to confidential information, customer relationships, and equity participation.

---

## 8. FIDUCIARY DUTIES AND CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

8.1 The Director must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to the Board promptly.

8.2 The Director may not hold directorships or significant interests in competing businesses without Board consent.

8.3 Related-party transactions (s75 Companies Act) require disclosure and Board approval.
