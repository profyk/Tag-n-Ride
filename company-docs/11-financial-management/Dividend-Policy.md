# DIVIDEND POLICY
## TAG-N-RIDE (PTY) LTD

**Ref:** TNR-FIN-DIV-POL-001
**Adopted by the Board:** [DATE]
**Review Frequency:** Annual or upon any material change in financial position
**Applicable Law:** Companies Act 71 of 2008 (s46); Income Tax Act 58 of 1962 (s64B–s64N)

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This policy sets out the framework within which the Board of Directors of Tag-n-Ride (Pty) Ltd ("**Company**") will consider the declaration and payment of dividends to shareholders.

---

## 2. POLICY STATEMENT

As an early-stage fintech company in a growth phase, Tag-n-Ride's primary objective is to **reinvest available profits into the platform, team, and market expansion** rather than distribute them as dividends.

The Board will not declare dividends until:
- The Company has achieved **consistent month-on-month revenue growth** for at least **6 consecutive months**
- The Company is **operationally cash flow positive** (excluding external funding)
- The Company maintains a **minimum cash reserve** equal to **6 months of operating expenses**
- All **SARS tax obligations** are current (no outstanding returns or debt)
- All **shareholder loan accounts** have been formally addressed (repaid or converted to equity)

---

## 3. SOLVENCY AND LIQUIDITY TEST (MANDATORY)

Before any dividend is declared, the Board must confirm — by resolution — that the Company satisfies the **solvency and liquidity test** as required by s46 of the Companies Act:

3.1 **Solvency:** Immediately after the distribution, the Company's assets (fairly valued) will equal or exceed its liabilities.

3.2 **Liquidity:** Immediately after the distribution, the Company will be able to pay its debts as they become due and payable in the ordinary course of business for a period of **12 months**.

Failure to satisfy this test renders any dividend declaration **void** and exposes directors to personal liability.

---

## 4. DECLARATION AUTHORITY

4.1 **Ordinary dividends** may be declared by the Board by **ordinary resolution** (majority of directors).

4.2 **Special distributions** (in excess of 50% of after-tax profits or involving return of capital) require a **special resolution** of shareholders (75% of voting rights).

4.3 Any dividend must be documented in a **formal Board Resolution** (see template TNR-TAX-DIV-001).

---

## 5. TIMING AND PAYMENT

- Dividends, once declared, must be paid within **[30] days** of the declaration date
- Record date for dividends will be set by the Board (typically 5–10 business days before payment date)
- Dividends will be paid by EFT to the registered banking details of each shareholder
- **Dividends Tax (DWT) at 20%** will be withheld from non-exempt shareholders and paid to SARS within **15 business days** via DTR02 on eFiling

---

## 6. PRO-RATA DISTRIBUTION

Dividends are paid **pro-rata to shareholding** unless the MOI or Shareholders Agreement specifies preferential dividend rights for a particular class of shares.

Current share classes:
- **Ordinary A shares:** Equal economic rights — all participate in dividends equally
- **ESOP options (unexercised):** Not entitled to dividends until options are exercised and shares are issued

---

## 7. REINVESTMENT OPTION

The Board may, at its discretion, offer shareholders a **dividend reinvestment option** (DRIP) — where the dividend is applied to purchase new shares at the then-current share price, subject to availability of authorised but unissued shares.

---

## 8. INVESTOR RIGHTS

Where existing shareholder agreements confer preferential dividend rights (e.g., participating preferred shares, cumulative preference dividends), those rights take precedence over this policy.

---

## 9. TAX TREATMENT

| Shareholder Type | Dividends Tax Rate |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| SA natural person | 20% |
| SA company (≥ 10% shareholding, resident) | 0% (exempt — s64F) |
| Foreign shareholder (subject to treaty) | Reduced rate (varies by DTA) |

Dividend declarations are reported on **DTR02** (SARS eFiling) within 15 business days of payment.

---

## 10. POLICY REVIEW

This policy is reviewed annually by the CEO and Board. Any amendment requires Board approval by ordinary resolution. Material changes (e.g., move to a regular dividend schedule) require notification to shareholders.
