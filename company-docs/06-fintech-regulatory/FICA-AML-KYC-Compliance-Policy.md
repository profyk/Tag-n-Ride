## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 Tag-n-Ride (Pty) Ltd ("the Company") operates a digital wallet and payment facilitation service. Depending on the nature and scope of our activities, we may qualify as an **Accountable Institution** under FICA or operate under an arrangement with a licensed accountable institution (such as Stitch).

1.2 Regardless of direct licensing status, the Company adopts the following AML/KYC procedures as best practice and as a condition of our Stitch payment gateway agreement.

1.3 This Policy applies to all employees, contractors, and anyone processing transactions on behalf of the Company.

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **AML** — Anti-Money Laundering
- **CTF** — Counter-Terrorism Financing
- **FIC** — Financial Intelligence Centre
- **FICA** — Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001
- **KYC** — Know Your Customer
- **PEP** — Politically Exposed Person
- **Suspicious Transaction** — any transaction where the Company has grounds to suspect money laundering or terrorism financing
- **Threshold Transaction** — a cash transaction or series of related transactions exceeding **R24,999.99**

---

## 3. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (KYC)

### 3.1 KYC Tiers

The Company applies a **risk-based tiered KYC approach**:

| Tier | Requirements | Transaction Limit |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Tier 1** | Mobile number + OTP verification | R500/day, R1,000 wallet max |
| **Tier 2** | SA ID number, selfie, document scan | R5,000/day, R25,000 wallet |
| **Tier 3** | Full ID, proof of address, source of funds | R25,000/day, R100,000 wallet |

### 3.2 Information to Collect

For **Tier 2 and above**, collect and verify:
  (a) Full legal name;
  (b) South African ID number (or passport number for non-SA nationals);
  (c) Date of birth;
  (d) Residential address;
  (e) Mobile number (verified via OTP);
  (f) Selfie/biometric check matched to ID document;
  (g) For drivers and fleet owners: proof of employment/vehicle documentation.

### 3.3 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

EDD is required for:
  (a) **PEPs** (Politically Exposed Persons) and their immediate family/associates;
  (b) Users from high-risk jurisdictions;
  (c) Unusual transaction patterns;
  (d) Accounts with high transaction volumes relative to stated income.

EDD steps include:
  (a) Source of funds declaration;
  (b) Source of wealth documentation;
  (c) Senior management approval for account activation.

## 4. ONGOING MONITORING

### 4.1 Transaction Monitoring

The Company shall monitor transactions for:
  (a) Unusual patterns — large, round-number transactions; multiple small transactions just below threshold;
  (b) Unusual frequency — significantly higher activity than typical user profile;
  (c) Unusual geography — transactions inconsistent with registered location;
  (d) Rapid wallet funding and withdrawal cycles.

### 4.2 Threshold Transaction Reporting

Any cash transaction (or series of related transactions in a 24-hour period) totalling **R24,999.99 or more** must be reported to the FIC as a **Cash Threshold Report (CTR)** within **2 days** of becoming aware.

### 4.3 Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)

4.3.1 If any employee has reason to suspect that a transaction relates to money laundering, terrorism financing, or proceeds of crime, they must:
  (a) Report immediately (verbally, then in writing within 24 hours) to the Compliance Officer;
  (b) NOT inform the customer of the suspicion (tipping off is a criminal offence);
  (c) The Compliance Officer shall file a **Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)** with the FIC via the goAML system within **15 days**.

### 4.4 Record Keeping

All KYC documents and transaction records must be retained for **5 years** from the date of the last transaction or account closure. Records must be readily available to the FIC or SARB upon request.

## 5. RISK ASSESSMENT

### 5.1 Customer Risk Scoring

Assign a risk rating to each customer at onboarding and review periodically:

| Risk Level | Profile |
|------------|---------|
| **Low** | Verified SA citizen, regular small fare payments, no alerts |
| **Medium** | Unverified details, irregular activity, foreign national |
| **High** | PEP, high-value flows, multiple failed verifications |

### 5.2 Prohibited Customers

Do not onboard or transact with:
  (a) Persons on OFAC, UN, or EU sanctions lists;
  (b) Entities designated as terrorist organisations under SA law;
  (c) Anonymous or fictitious accounts.

---

## 6. STAFF TRAINING

6.1 All employees who have access to customer accounts or transaction data must complete **FICA/AML training** upon joining and annually thereafter.

6.2 Training topics include:
  (a) Identifying red flags for money laundering and fraud;
  (b) How to report a suspicious transaction;
  (c) POPIA obligations relating to KYC data;
  (d) Consequences of non-compliance.

6.3 Training completion must be documented and records maintained.

---

## 7. NON-COMPLIANCE AND PENALTIES

7.1 Failure to comply with FICA obligations exposes the Company and responsible individuals to:
  (a) Administrative sanctions from the FIC;
  (b) Criminal prosecution under FICA (up to 15 years imprisonment and/or significant fines);
  (c) Loss of operating licences.

7.2 Employees who knowingly facilitate prohibited transactions or fail to report suspicious activity will face immediate dismissal and referral to law enforcement.

---

## 8. COMPLIANCE OFFICER RESPONSIBILITIES

The Compliance Officer shall:
  (a) Maintain and update this Policy;
  (b) Ensure KYC processes are implemented and functioning;
  (c) File all required reports with the FIC;
  (d) Conduct staff training;
  (e) Respond to FIC requests and enquiries;
  (f) Report compliance status to the Board quarterly.
