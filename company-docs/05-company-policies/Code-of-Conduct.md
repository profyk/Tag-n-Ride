## TAG-N-RIDE (PTY) LTD

## PREAMBLE

Tag-n-Ride (Pty) Ltd ("the Company") is a fintech company serving South African commuters, drivers, and fleet owners. We handle people's money, personal data, and livelihoods. The trust our users place in us is the foundation of everything we do.

This Code of Conduct applies to **every person** associated with Tag-n-Ride — employees, directors, co-founders, contractors, consultants, investors, and anyone acting on behalf of the Company.

Compliance with this Code is not optional. Violations may result in disciplinary action, termination, or legal proceedings.

---

## PART 1 — INTEGRITY AND ETHICS

### 1.1 Honesty

We tell the truth — to our users, our investors, our partners, and each other. No misleading claims, inflated metrics, or concealed problems.

### 1.2 Transparency

If you make a mistake, own it early. Attempting to conceal an error is a more serious offence than the error itself.

### 1.3 No Fraud or Misrepresentation

Zero tolerance for fraud, falsification of records, manipulating financial data, or misrepresenting the Company's performance to any internal or external stakeholder.

### 1.4 Anti-Bribery and Corruption

- No employee, contractor, or agent may offer, pay, accept, or solicit bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments in any form.
- This applies to interactions with private parties, government officials, and regulatory bodies.
- Gifts from third parties must not exceed **R500** in value and must be declared to your manager.
- Any request for a bribe or inappropriate payment must be reported immediately.
- Tag-n-Ride complies with the Prevention and Combating of Corrupt Activities Act (PRECCA) 12 of 2004.

---

## PART 2 — RESPECT AND INCLUSION

### 2.1 Zero Tolerance for Harassment

Harassment of any kind is prohibited — sexual, racial, religious, gender-based, or any other form. This includes:
  (a) Unwanted physical contact;
  (b) Sexual or offensive language, images, or jokes;
  (c) Bullying, intimidation, or humiliation;
  (d) Online harassment via company or personal channels related to work.

Reports of harassment will be investigated immediately. The identity of the complainant will be protected to the extent possible.

### 2.2 Discrimination

Tag-n-Ride does not discriminate on the basis of race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, national origin, or any other protected characteristic. All decisions on hiring, promotion, discipline, and termination shall be based solely on merit, performance, and business need.

### 2.3 Respect in Communication

Treat everyone — colleagues, users, drivers, partners, and regulators — with dignity and respect, regardless of role or seniority. Aggressive, condescending, or disrespectful communication has no place here.

---

## PART 3 — CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION

### 3.1 Company Confidentiality

All employees and contractors must protect the Company's trade secrets, technical systems, financial data, business strategies, and investor information. Unauthorised disclosure is a dismissible offence.

### 3.2 User Data Protection

Tag-n-Ride operates under the **Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA)**. Every person at Tag-n-Ride has an obligation to:
  (a) Access only the user data necessary for their specific role;
  (b) Never export, copy, or share user data without explicit authorisation;
  (c) Never use user data for personal benefit or curiosity;
  (d) Report any suspected data breach immediately to the Information Officer.

### 3.3 Financial Data Integrity

The Company handles real money flows — ride payments, wallet top-ups, and driver cashouts. Any tampering with, manipulation of, or unauthorised access to financial records or transactions is a criminal offence and will be prosecuted.

---

## PART 4 — CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

### 4.1 Disclosure

A conflict of interest arises when your personal interests, financial interests, or outside relationships could influence — or appear to influence — your decisions at Tag-n-Ride. All conflicts of interest must be **declared in writing** to the CEO or Board immediately upon becoming aware.

### 4.2 Common Conflicts to Disclose

  (a) Owning shares in or working for a competitor;
  (b) Having a personal relationship with a supplier, vendor, or job applicant;
  (c) Receiving gifts or entertainment above policy limits from partners;
  (d) Directing Company business to a company in which you have a personal interest.

### 4.3 Resolution

Declared conflicts will be reviewed by the CEO or Board. You may be required to recuse yourself from affected decisions. Undeclared conflicts discovered after the fact are treated as misconduct.

---

## PART 5 — USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES

### 5.1 Company Assets

Company property (devices, software, vehicles, funds) is for business purposes. Limited incidental personal use is permitted but must not:
  (a) Be excessive or impact productivity;
  (b) Expose the Company to security or legal risk;
  (c) Involve accessing inappropriate content.

### 5.2 Intellectual Property

All work created in the course of employment belongs to Tag-n-Ride. Do not use Company IP for personal projects or share it with third parties without authorisation.

### 5.3 Financial Integrity

- Never approve expenses for yourself without a second authoriser.
- Never submit false or inflated expense claims.
- Never make or approve payments outside the authorised process.
- The CEO must approve all payments over R50,000.

---

## PART 6 — SOCIAL MEDIA AND PUBLIC COMMUNICATIONS

### 6.1 Personal Social Media

When posting publicly about your work at Tag-n-Ride:
  (a) Do not disclose confidential or non-public information;
  (b) Make clear when expressing personal opinions that they are your own and not Tag-n-Ride's official position;
  (c) Do not make disparaging remarks about Tag-n-Ride, competitors, users, or colleagues.

### 6.2 Authorised Spokespersons

Only the CEO, or persons expressly authorised by the CEO, may make public statements on behalf of Tag-n-Ride, including media interviews, press releases, and official social media posts.

### 6.3 Compliance with Platform Rules

All communications on behalf of the Company must comply with applicable laws, including the Consumer Protection Act, the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act, and POPIA.

## PART 7 — HEALTH, SAFETY, AND WELLBEING

7.1 We are committed to a safe, healthy work environment.
7.2 You must not work while under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or any impairing substance.
7.3 Report any workplace hazards or unsafe conditions immediately.
7.4 Tag-n-Ride supports employee mental health — reach out to your manager or use available support resources if you are struggling.

---

## PART 8 — REPORTING VIOLATIONS

### 8.1 Your Obligation

If you become aware of a violation of this Code, you have an obligation to report it. Silence in the face of known misconduct is itself a breach of this Code.

### 8.2 How to Report

- **Direct report to CEO:** [EMAIL]
- **Anonymous reporting:** [MECHANISM — e.g., anonymous email or third-party tool]
- **Serious allegations involving the CEO:** Escalate to the Board of Directors.

### 8.3 No Retaliation

Tag-n-Ride strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who in good faith reports a violation of this Code or cooperates with an investigation. Any act of retaliation is itself a serious disciplinary offence.

### 8.4 Investigations

All reports will be investigated promptly, fairly, and confidentially. False reports made maliciously are also a violation of this Code.

---

## PART 9 — CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATIONS

Violations of this Code may result in:
- Formal warning;
- Suspension pending investigation;
- Dismissal without notice (for gross misconduct);
- Civil or criminal prosecution, where applicable.

The severity of the consequence will be proportionate to the nature, intent, and impact of the violation.
