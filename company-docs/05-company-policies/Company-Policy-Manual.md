## POLICY 1 — FINANCIAL AUTHORITY AND CONTROLS

### 1.1 Spending Authorisation Matrix

| Transaction Value | Authorisation Required |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Up to R5,000 | Department Head |
| R5,001 – R50,000 | CEO |
| R50,001 – R200,000 | CEO + one Board member |
| Over R200,000 | Full Board resolution |
| Any related-party transaction | CEO + Board approval |

### 1.2 Bank Account Management

- The Company maintains a primary business account and a **segregated trust/user-funds account** for wallet float.
- All bank account signatories must be approved by Board resolution.
- Online banking access requires dual authorisation for payments over R10,000.
- Bank statements shall be reconciled monthly by the Finance function.

### 1.3 Payment Processing Accounts

- Stitch account credentials are held by the CTO and Finance Officer only.
- API keys and payment gateway credentials must be rotated every **6 months**.
- Unused test credentials must be deactivated.

### 1.4 Petty Cash

- Petty cash float shall not exceed **R2,000**.
- All petty cash disbursements require receipts and approval by a manager.
- Petty cash is reconciled monthly.

### 1.5 Procurement Policy

- All significant procurement (over R10,000) requires at least **2 (two) competing quotes**.
- Preferred suppliers must be approved by the CEO.
- No verbal commitments to suppliers — all commitments must be in writing.

## POLICY 2 — HUMAN RESOURCES

### 2.1 Recruitment

- All vacancies require CEO approval before advertising.
- Job descriptions must accurately reflect the role requirements.
- Shortlisting and interviews must assess merit and role fit only.
- Reference checks are mandatory for all final candidates.
- Background checks (credit, criminal) are required for finance, technology, and senior roles.

### 2.2 Onboarding

New employees must complete, on Day 1:
  (a) Signed employment contract;
  (b) Signed NDA and IP Assignment;
  (c) Signed Code of Conduct acknowledgement;
  (d) Bank details and tax number form;
  (e) POPIA training briefing.

### 2.3 Remuneration Review

- Salary reviews are conducted annually in [MONTH].
- Merit increases are based on performance review outcomes.
- Any salary increase must be approved by the CEO and documented in writing.

### 2.4 Retrenchment and Restructuring

Retrenchment shall comply with Section 189 of the Labour Relations Act, including:
  (a) Written notice to affected employees;
  (b) Genuine consultation with affected employees;
  (c) Consideration of alternatives to retrenchment;
  (d) Severance pay as required by the BCEA.

## POLICY 3 — INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY AND SECURITY

### 3.1 Access Management

- All system access is role-based ("least privilege principle").
- Access rights must be approved by the CTO (Mr Profy T D Keakile).
- Employee access must be revoked within **4 hours** of termination.
- Annual access rights review is mandatory.

### 3.2 Data Classification

| Level | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Public** | Can be shared freely | Marketing materials, app store listings |
| **Internal** | For employees only | Internal procedures, product roadmaps |
| **Confidential** | Restricted access | Financial data, API keys, investor info |
| **Restricted** | Strictly controlled | User PII, wallet balances, payment credentials |

### 3.3 Incident Response (Summary)

*(Full policy in Fintech-Regulatory folder)*

- All security incidents must be reported to the CTO within **1 hour**.
- Data breaches affecting user data must be reported to the CIPC/Information Regulator within **72 hours** per POPIA.
- Post-incident reviews are mandatory.

### 3.4 Software Development Policy

- All production deployments require code review by at least one other developer.
- No direct commits to `main` or `production` branches — all changes via pull requests.
- Security testing (OWASP principles) must be applied to all payment-related code.
- Production credentials and API keys must be stored in secure environment variables — never hardcoded.

## POLICY 4 — HEALTH AND SAFETY

### 4.1 General

- Tag-n-Ride complies with the Occupational Health and Safety Act 85 of 1993 ("OHSA").
- A Health and Safety Representative shall be appointed once the Company reaches **10 employees**.

### 4.2 Remote Work Safety

- Employees working remotely are responsible for ensuring their workspace is safe and ergonomic.
- The Company may provide an ergonomic assessment on request.

### 4.3 Substance Policy

No employee shall work while impaired by alcohol, illegal substances, or prescribed medication that affects performance. Violation is gross misconduct.

## POLICY 5 — ENVIRONMENTAL AND SUSTAINABILITY POLICY

5.1 Tag-n-Ride is a digital-first company with a minimal physical footprint.

5.2 We commit to:
  (a) Paperless operations wherever possible;
  (b) Using cloud infrastructure hosted in energy-efficient data centres;
  (c) Reducing travel through the use of digital tools;
  (d) Supporting South African taxi industry sustainability by reducing cash handling risks.
