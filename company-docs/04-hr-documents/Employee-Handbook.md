## SECTION 3 — LEAVE POLICY

### 3.1 Annual Leave
- 21 consecutive days (15 working days) per year.
- Apply for leave at least **2 weeks in advance** via [leave system/email to manager].
- Leave shall not be unreasonably denied.
- Carry-over of more than 10 working days requires management approval.

### 3.2 Sick Leave
- 30 days over a 3-year cycle.
- Notify your manager **before 9:00 AM** on the day you are sick.
- A doctor's certificate is required for absences of more than 2 consecutive days.

### 3.3 Family Responsibility Leave
- 3 days per year for qualifying events (birth of child, child illness, death of immediate family).
- Must be accompanied by supporting documentation.

### 3.4 Study/Exam Leave
- Employees enrolled in relevant courses may apply for study leave, subject to management approval and relevance to the role.

### 3.5 Leave Without Pay
- May be granted at management's discretion after all paid leave is exhausted.

### 4.1 Probation
- All new employees undergo a 3-month probation.
- During probation, formal check-ins will be held at month 1 and month 3.
- Probation may be extended by one month if performance is borderline.

### 4.2 Performance Reviews
- Formal annual review in [MONTH].
- Informal quarterly check-ins with your manager.

### 4.3 Learning and Development

## SECTION 5 — CONDUCT AND DISCIPLINE

### 5.1 Code of Conduct Summary
*(Full Code of Conduct is a separate document)*

- Act with **honesty and integrity** at all times.
- Treat colleagues, customers, and partners with **respect**.
- Protect all **confidential information** of the Company and its users.
- Do not engage in **bribery, corruption, or fraud**.
- Declare any **conflicts of interest** promptly.
- Do not engage in **harassment**, bullying, or discrimination.

### 5.2 Disciplinary Process

Tag-n-Ride follows a fair and consistent disciplinary process aligned with the Labour Relations Act.

**Steps:**
1. **Informal counselling** — for minor issues.
2. **Written warning** — for repeated minor misconduct.
3. **Final written warning** — for serious or repeated misconduct.
4. **Dismissal** — for gross misconduct or failure to improve.

Employees have the right to:
  - Be informed of the charge;
  - Prepare and present their case;
  - Have a colleague or union representative present;
  - Appeal a decision.

### 5.3 Gross Misconduct (Immediate Dismissal)
- Theft or fraud involving the Company or its users.
- Accessing or misusing payment or financial data.
- Disclosure of confidential or user data.
- Deliberate sabotage of Company systems.
- Sexual harassment or assault.
- Falsification of records or documents.

## SECTION 6 — TECHNOLOGY AND DATA USE

### 6.1 Company Devices and Accounts

- Company-issued devices and software licences are for business use.
- Limited personal use is permitted, provided it does not interfere with work or create security risks.
- Employees shall not install unauthorised software on Company devices.

### 6.2 Data Protection (POPIA)

Tag-n-Ride handles sensitive financial and personal data. All employees must:
  - Access only the data necessary for their role.
  - Never share, export, or copy user data without authorisation.
  - Report any suspected data breach to the Information Officer within **1 hour** of discovery.
  - Never use user data for personal purposes.
  - Complete any required POPIA training.

### 6.3 Password and Security

- Use strong, unique passwords for all Company accounts.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever available.
- Never share login credentials with colleagues.
- Lock your screen when leaving your workstation.

### 6.4 Acceptable Use

Company systems may **not** be used to:
  - Access illegal, pornographic, or harmful content;
  - Conduct personal business activities;
  - Send discriminatory or offensive communications;
  - Attempt to breach security systems.

## SECTION 9 — GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

If you have a grievance:
1. **Speak directly** with the person involved where it is safe to do so.
2. **Escalate to your manager** if unresolved.
3. **Submit a written grievance** to the CEO if the matter remains unresolved.
4. If the matter involves the CEO, raise it with a Board member or the CCMA.

All grievances will be treated with confidentiality and without victimisation.
