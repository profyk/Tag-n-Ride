## 3. REMUNERATION

### 3.1 Basic Salary

3.1.1 The Employee shall receive a gross monthly salary of **R[AMOUNT]** per month.

3.1.2 Salary shall be paid on the **last working day** of each month by EFT into the Employee's nominated bank account.

### 3.2 Annual Review

3.2.1 Salary shall be reviewed annually at the Company's discretion, taking into account performance and Company profitability. Review is not an automatic increase.

### 3.3 Deductions

3.3.1 The Company shall deduct from the Employee's salary:
  (a) PAYE (Pay-As-You-Earn) as required by SARS;
  (b) UIF contributions (1% Employee, 1% Employer);
  (c) Any other statutory deductions;
  (d) Any deductions authorised in writing by the Employee.

---

## 4. WORKING HOURS

4.1 Normal working hours are **Monday to Friday, [START TIME] to [END TIME]**, with a **1-hour** lunch break.

4.2 The Employee may be required to work reasonable additional hours to meet business needs. Overtime shall be compensated in accordance with the Basic Conditions of Employment Act 75 of 1997 ("BCEA").

4.3 For employees earning above the BCEA earnings threshold, overtime is by agreement and may be compensated via time off in lieu or additional pay at the Company's discretion.

---

## 5. LEAVE

### 5.1 Annual Leave

The Employee is entitled to **21 (twenty-one) consecutive days** annual leave per cycle (equivalent to 15 working days), in accordance with the BCEA.

Annual leave shall be taken at a time mutually agreed between the Employee and the Company. Leave may not be accumulated beyond **30 working days** without Company approval.

### 5.2 Sick Leave

The Employee is entitled to **30 (thirty) days** sick leave per 3-year cycle, in accordance with the BCEA. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding **2 (two) consecutive days** or after **2 uncertified sick days** in a month.

### 5.3 Family Responsibility Leave

The Employee is entitled to **3 (three) days** family responsibility leave per year for:
  (a) The birth of a child;
  (b) Illness of a child;
  (c) Death of a spouse, life partner, parent, adoptive parent, grandparent, child, adopted child, grandchild, or sibling.

### 5.4 Maternity/Parental Leave

Maternity leave shall be in accordance with the BCEA and applicable UIF provisions. The Company shall contribute to UIF and assist with UIF claims.

### 5.5 Public Holidays

The Employee is entitled to all South African public holidays as declared under the Public Holidays Act 36 of 1994.

## 7. DUTIES AND PERFORMANCE

7.1 The Employee shall:
  (a) Perform their duties diligently, competently, and in good faith;
  (b) Follow all lawful instructions of the Company;
  (c) Report to **[REPORTING MANAGER / CEO]**;
  (d) Meet performance targets as communicated and reviewed by management;
  (e) Comply with all Company policies, procedures, and codes of conduct.

7.2 Performance shall be formally reviewed **annually** and informally on an ongoing basis.

---

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 The Employee shall keep strictly confidential all proprietary information, trade secrets, client data, business strategies, financial information, source code, and technical systems of the Company.

8.2 This obligation applies during employment and for **5 (five) years** after termination, for any reason.

8.3 On termination, the Employee shall immediately return all Company property and data.

---

## 9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

9.1 All work product, inventions, code, designs, processes, and any other Intellectual Property created by the Employee in the course of employment shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

9.2 The Employee irrevocably assigns to the Company all rights in such Intellectual Property.

9.3 This obligation applies even if work is performed outside of normal working hours, provided it relates to the Company's Business.

---

## 10. NON-SOLICITATION

During employment and for **12 (twelve) months** after termination:

10.1 The Employee shall not solicit or entice away any employee or contractor of the Company.

10.2 The Employee shall not solicit any client, customer, driver, or partner of the Company for a competing business.

---

## 11. SECONDARY EMPLOYMENT

11.1 The Employee shall not, without the prior written consent of the Company, engage in any other employment, business, or paid activity that:
  (a) Creates a conflict of interest with the Company's Business;
  (b) Requires the Employee to use Company resources or time.

---

## 12. COMPANY PROPERTY

12.1 Any equipment, device, software access, or materials provided by the Company shall:
  (a) Remain the property of the Company;
  (b) Be used only for Company business purposes;
  (c) Be returned in good condition upon termination.

---

## 13. DISCIPLINARY AND GRIEVANCE

13.1 The Company shall apply a fair and consistent disciplinary process in accordance with the Labour Relations Act 66 of 1995 ("LRA") and the Company's Disciplinary Code.

13.2 The Employee has the right to raise grievances in accordance with the Company's Grievance Procedure.

---

## 14. TERMINATION

### 14.1 Notice Period

| Service Period | Notice Required |
|----------------|-----------------|
| During probation | 1 week |
| 0–1 year | 2 weeks |
| 1–5 years | 4 weeks |
| Over 5 years | 4 weeks |

### 14.2 Summary Dismissal

The Company may terminate employment without notice for gross misconduct, including but not limited to:
  (a) Theft, fraud, or dishonesty;
  (b) Wilful damage to Company property;
  (c) Disclosure of confidential information;
  (d) Serious assault or harassment;
  (e) Consistent and material non-performance after a fair process.

### 14.3 Retrenchment

In the event of retrenchment, the Company shall follow the consultation requirements of Section 189 of the LRA.

---

15.1 This Contract is governed by the laws of South Africa, including the BCEA, LRA, and applicable SARS regulations.

15.2 This Contract may only be amended in writing, signed by both Parties.

15.3 If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force.
