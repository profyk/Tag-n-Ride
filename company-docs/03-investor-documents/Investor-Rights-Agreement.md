## 1. INFORMATION AND REPORTING RIGHTS

### 1.1 Major Investor Rights (holding ≥ 5% of Shares)

Each Major Investor shall receive:

  (a) **Monthly** — Management accounts (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow) within 15 days of month-end;
  (b) **Quarterly** — Business update report including KPIs: active users, transaction volume (GMV), revenue, burn rate, runway;
  (c) **Annually** — Audited financial statements within 90 days of financial year-end;
  (d) **Ad hoc** — Immediate notification of any material adverse change, litigation, regulatory action, or data breach.

### 1.2 All Investors

All Investors shall receive:
  (a) **Annually** — Audited financial statements;
  (b) **Annually** — Investor newsletter or update.

---

## 2. INSPECTION RIGHTS

2.1 Any Major Investor may, upon **10 (ten) business days** written notice, inspect the Company's:
  (a) Financial records and books of account;
  (b) Physical premises and equipment;
  (c) Material contracts (subject to confidentiality obligations to third parties).

2.2 Inspections shall take place during normal business hours and shall not unreasonably disrupt operations.

---

## 3. PRE-EMPTIVE RIGHTS ON NEW ISSUANCES

3.1 Before issuing new Shares in any future round, the Company shall offer each Major Investor the right to subscribe for their **pro-rata share** of the new issuance.

3.2 The Company shall deliver a written notice specifying:
  (a) Number of new Shares being issued;
  (b) Issue price per Share;
  (c) Proposed use of proceeds;
  (d) Deadline to exercise (minimum 15 business days).

3.3 Pre-emptive rights do not apply to:
  (a) ESOP shares;
  (b) Shares issued in an approved Exit Event;
  (c) Shares issued to strategic partners with Board approval.

---

## 4. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL AND CO-SALE

### 4.1 Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

If any Shareholder (including the Founder) proposes to sell Shares to a third party:
  (a) The Company shall have a first right to purchase those Shares at the proposed price;
  (b) If the Company declines, Major Investors shall have a secondary ROFR, pro-rata to their shareholding.
  (c) ROFR must be exercised within **15 business days** of written notice.

### 4.2 Co-Sale Rights (Tag-Along)

If the Founder proposes to sell **20% or more** of their Shares to a third party and neither the Company nor Investors exercise the ROFR, each Major Investor shall have the right to sell their Shares in the same transaction, on the same terms, pro-rata to their shareholding.

---

## 5. DRAG-ALONG

5.1 If Shareholders holding **75% or more** of all Shares approve a sale of the Company, all remaining Shareholders (including Investors) shall be required to sell their Shares to the same buyer on the same terms.

5.2 The drag-along obligation is subject to:
  (a) All Shareholders receiving the same per-share consideration, adjusted for liquidation preferences;
  (b) Representations required of dragged Shareholders being limited to title, authority, and absence of encumbrances.

---

## 6. REGISTRATION RIGHTS (FOR FUTURE IPO)

6.1 Investors holding **5% or more** shall have the right to require the Company to include their Shares in any IPO registration or listing (piggyback registration rights), subject to underwriter lock-up requirements.

6.2 These rights shall be subject to a **180-day post-IPO lock-up period**.

---

## 7. BOARD OBSERVER RIGHTS

Any Investor holding **5% or more** but not entitled to a Board seat shall have the right to:
  (a) Attend all Board meetings as a non-voting observer;
  (b) Receive all Board materials and minutes simultaneously with directors.

---

## 8. ANTI-DILUTION PROTECTION

8.1 If the Company issues Shares at a price **below** the price paid by any Investor in this round ("**Down Round**"), that Investor's effective purchase price shall be adjusted using the **weighted average anti-dilution** formula.

8.2 The Company shall issue additional Shares to affected Investors (at no cost) to reflect the adjusted purchase price.

8.3 Anti-dilution does not apply to:
  (a) ESOP shares;
  (b) Shares issued in an Exit Event at a premium to the last round price.

---

## 9. PROTECTIVE PROVISIONS

The following actions require the affirmative vote of **Investors holding a majority** of Investor Shares:

  (a) Amendment to this Agreement or the Company's MOI in a manner that adversely affects Investors;
  (b) Issuance of any shares senior to or pari passu with Investor preference shares;
  (c) Declaration of dividends;
  (d) Winding up or liquidation of the Company;
  (e) Acquisition or disposal of assets with a value exceeding **R5,000,000**;
  (f) Incurring debt exceeding **R2,000,000** in aggregate;
  (g) Any related-party transaction exceeding **R200,000**.

---

## 10. CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 All Investors agree to keep confidential all non-public information about the Company received under this Agreement.

10.2 This obligation survives the transfer of Shares for **3 (three) years**.

---

## 11. TERMINATION

This Agreement terminates upon the earliest of:
  (a) An Exit Event (IPO or trade sale);
  (b) Winding up of the Company;
  (c) Agreement of all Parties in writing.

---

## 12. GOVERNING LAW

Governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Disputes resolved by arbitration per AFSA rules.
