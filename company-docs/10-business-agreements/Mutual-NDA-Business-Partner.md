## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"**Confidential Information**" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("**Disclosing Party**") to the other ("**Receiving Party**"), whether in writing, orally, electronically, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, financial forecasts, and projections
- Technology, software, source code, algorithms, platform architecture
- Customer lists, user data, pricing, and commercial terms
- Investor information, cap tables, fundraising plans
- Regulatory filings, licence applications, legal strategies
- Personnel information, compensation structures
- Any information marked "Confidential" or which a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the context of disclosure

---

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to:

2.1 Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential and not disclose it to any third party without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent.

2.2 Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose stated above and for no other purpose.

2.3 Limit access to Confidential Information to those employees, directors, and advisors (a) who have a need to know for the Purpose and (b) who are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

2.4 Apply at least the same degree of care to protect the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information as it applies to its own confidential information, and in all cases no less than **reasonable care**.

2.5 Notify the Disclosing Party promptly upon becoming aware of any unauthorised disclosure or use of Confidential Information.

---

## 3. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure (with evidence);
(c) Is lawfully obtained from a third party not bound by confidentiality;
(d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Confidential Information; or
(e) Must be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory requirement — in which case the Receiving Party shall give maximum possible prior written notice to the Disclosing Party to allow it to seek a protective order.

---

## 4. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

Upon request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement:
- The Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all Confidential Information (including copies, notes, and electronic records)
- Written certification of destruction shall be provided within **5 business days** if requested

---

## 5. NO LICENCE OR OBLIGATION

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any right, licence, or interest in the Confidential Information of the other Party, or any intellectual property rights.

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to enter into any commercial arrangement, proceed with the Purpose, or make any disclosure.
