## PART 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes:

1.1.1 Standards of conduct for all taxi operators ("**Operators**") and taxi drivers ("**Drivers**") registered on the Tag-n-Ride Platform;

1.1.2 Obligations regarding passenger conduct, fair treatment, and service standards;

1.1.3 Payment and financial obligations specific to the Platform;

1.1.4 Fraud prevention requirements and reporting obligations;

1.1.5 The process for raising, investigating, and resolving disputes;

1.1.6 The conditions under which a Platform account may be suspended or banned.

### 1.2 Scope

This Code applies to:

- All registered Operators (individual taxi owners and fleet owners)
- All registered Drivers
- Any person authorised by an Operator to use the Platform on their behalf

### 1.3 Legal Basis

This Code of Conduct operates within the framework of:

- National Land Transport Act 5 of 2009 ("**NLTA**")
- Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 ("**CPA**")
- Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001 ("**FICA**")
- Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("**POPIA**")
- Prevention and Combating of Corrupt Activities Act 12 of 2004 ("**PRECCA**")
- National Road Traffic Act 93 of 1996

## PART 2: CONDUCT STANDARDS — PASSENGER TREATMENT

### 2.1 Right to Pay Digitally

2.1.1 All registered Operators and Drivers must accept digital fare payments through the Tag-n-Ride Platform from any passenger who wishes to pay digitally.

2.1.2 No Driver or Operator may:

(a) Refuse to accept a valid digital payment from a passenger;

(b) Charge a higher fare for digital payment than for cash payment;

(c) Demand that a passenger pay cash if the Platform is available and functional;

(d) Intimidate, threaten, or harass a passenger for choosing to pay digitally.

2.1.3 Where the Platform is genuinely unavailable due to a technical outage, Drivers must inform passengers politely and accept alternative payment. Such outages must be reported to Tag-n-Ride support immediately.

### 2.2 Passenger Safety and Dignity

2.2.1 All passengers must be treated with dignity and respect at all times, regardless of race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, or any other characteristic protected under the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa, 1996.

2.2.2 Drivers shall not:

(a) Use abusive, threatening, or offensive language towards passengers;

(b) Overload a vehicle beyond its licensed carrying capacity as per the NLTA and National Road Traffic Act 93 of 1996;

(c) Drive recklessly or under the influence of alcohol or drugs;

(d) Use a mobile phone while driving.

2.2.3 Where a passenger appears to be in distress, vulnerable, or requires assistance, Drivers shall act in a manner consistent with reasonable care.

### 2.3 Refunds and Overcharges

2.3.1 If a passenger is overcharged through the Platform due to a technical error or Driver error, the Operator must facilitate a refund through the Tag-n-Ride Platform within 48 hours of the complaint being lodged.

2.3.2 This obligation exists independently of the broader dispute process and arises from the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008, which entitles consumers to fair pricing and redress for overcharging.

## PART 3: PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Legitimate Fare Collection

3.1.1 Operators and Drivers may only collect fares through the Platform for genuine transport services rendered on NLTA-authorised routes.

3.1.2 Fares charged through the Platform must correspond to the applicable route tariff or association-approved fare schedule. Charging above the approved fare constitutes a violation of this Code and may constitute a contravention of the CPA.

3.1.3 Drivers shall ensure the QR Code Device is accessible to passengers at all times during operation.

### 3.2 QR Code Integrity

3.2.1 Drivers must use only the authorised QR Code Device issued for their assigned vehicle. No Driver may:

(a) Use a QR Code Device from another vehicle;

(b) Display an altered, copied, or counterfeit QR code;

(c) Direct passengers to scan any QR code other than the Tag-n-Ride-issued device.

3.2.2 Tampering with a QR Code Device is a serious violation and will result in immediate account suspension (see Part 6).

### 3.3 Digital Wallet Obligations

3.3.1 Operators must maintain accurate banking details in their Digital Wallet account at all times.

3.3.2 Operators shall not use the Digital Wallet to receive funds that are not attributable to legitimate taxi fare payments.

3.3.3 Operators must not attempt to circumvent Platform fee deductions through split transactions, fictitious refunds, or any other manipulation of the system.

## PART 4: FRAUD PREVENTION

### 4.1 Zero Tolerance for Fraud

Tag-n-Ride has zero tolerance for fraud. Any form of fraudulent conduct on the Platform will result in immediate suspension, permanent deregistration, and referral to the South African Police Service ("**SAPS**") and the Financial Intelligence Centre ("**FIC**") where applicable.

### 4.2 Prohibited Fraudulent Conduct

The following conduct constitutes fraud and is strictly prohibited:

| Fraudulent Act | Description |
|---|---|
| Fictitious transactions | Processing payments for trips that did not occur |
| QR code spoofing | Using a fake or cloned QR code to divert passenger payments |
| Identity fraud | Registering on the Platform using another person's identity |
| Double charging | Charging a passenger twice for the same trip |
| Collusion fraud | Working with passengers or third parties to generate false transactions |
| Chargeback abuse | Deliberately inducing chargebacks to extract funds from the Platform |
| Money laundering | Using the Platform to launder funds in contravention of FICA |
| Licence fraud | Registering vehicles under NLTA licences that do not apply to those vehicles |

### 4.3 Reporting Obligation

4.3.1 Any Operator or Driver who becomes aware of fraudulent activity by another Operator, Driver, or third party must report it to Tag-n-Ride immediately via the reporting channels specified in Part 5.

4.3.2 Tag-n-Ride maintains a confidential fraud reporting hotline: [FRAUD HOTLINE NUMBER / EMAIL].

4.3.3 Reports made in good faith shall be treated confidentially. No Operator or Driver shall be victimised, harassed, or disadvantaged for reporting fraud in good faith.

### 4.4 Transaction Monitoring

Tag-n-Ride employs automated transaction monitoring systems to detect unusual patterns. Operators and Drivers consent to such monitoring as a condition of Platform access.

## PART 5: DISPUTE PROCESS

### 5.1 Types of Disputes

Disputes that may be raised under this Code include:

5.1.1 **Operator-initiated disputes:** Incorrect settlement amounts, Platform errors, QR code malfunctions;

5.1.2 **Passenger complaints:** Overcharging, refusal to accept digital payment, poor conduct;

5.1.3 **Driver disputes:** Disagreements regarding payment splits, wallet access, or Driver account issues;

5.1.4 **Inter-operator disputes:** Allegations of unfair competition, route encroachment, or fraud.

### 5.2 Step 1 — Direct Resolution

5.2.1 Disputes between Operators and Drivers should first be resolved directly between the parties involved within five (5) business days.

5.2.2 Passenger complaints should first be addressed by the Operator within 48 hours of the complaint being received.

### 5.3 Step 2 — Tag-n-Ride Support

5.3.1 If direct resolution fails, the dispute must be submitted to Tag-n-Ride's Support Team using the following channels:

| Channel | Contact |
|---|---|
| In-app dispute form | Tag-n-Ride Operator App → Support |
| Email | support@tagnride.co.za |
| Phone | [SUPPORT LINE NUMBER] |

5.3.2 The following information must be provided with every dispute submission:

- Full name and Operator / Driver ID
- Date and time of the incident
- Vehicle registration number
- Transaction reference number (if applicable)
- Description of the dispute
- Supporting evidence (screenshots, receipts, etc.)

### 5.4 Step 3 — Formal Investigation

5.4.1 Tag-n-Ride's compliance team shall acknowledge receipt of a formal dispute within two (2) business days.

5.4.2 A decision shall be issued within fifteen (15) business days of receiving all relevant information.

5.4.3 During an active investigation, Tag-n-Ride may temporarily suspend the affected account(s) where there is a risk of further harm or evidence loss.

### 5.5 Step 4 — Association Escalation

5.5.1 If an Operator is dissatisfied with Tag-n-Ride's decision, the dispute may be escalated to the relevant taxi association for review.

5.5.2 The taxi association shall designate a dispute committee to review escalated disputes.

5.5.3 The association's decision shall be advisory unless the Parties have agreed to binding association-level dispute resolution in their partnership agreement.

### 5.6 Step 5 — Formal Dispute Resolution

Unresolved disputes may be referred to AFSA mediation and, if necessary, AFSA binding arbitration, as set out in the Operator's Onboarding Agreement.

## PART 6: SUSPENSION AND BAN CONDITIONS

### 6.1 Suspension (Temporary Account Restriction)

Tag-n-Ride may suspend a Platform account on a temporary basis pending investigation or as a sanction for the following:

| Ground | Duration |
|---|---|
| First-time minor Code violation (e.g., QR Code Display failure) | 3 days |
| Passenger complaint upheld (overcharging) | 7 days or until refund confirmed |
| Unresolved KYC re-verification | Until remedied |
| Ongoing FICA compliance investigation | Until resolved |
| Single instance of suspected fraud (pending investigation) | Until investigation complete |

### 6.2 Permanent Deregistration (Ban)

The following conduct will result in immediate permanent deregistration from the Platform with no possibility of reinstatement:

6.2.1 **Confirmed fraud** — any proven instance of fictitious transactions, QR code spoofing, identity fraud, or money laundering;

6.2.2 **Violent conduct** — physical assault or credible threat of violence against a passenger, Tag-n-Ride employee, or other operator/driver;

6.2.3 **Repeat serious violations** — three (3) or more serious violations within any twelve (12)-month period;

6.2.4 **Criminal conviction** — conviction for any offence under PRECCA, FICA, the National Payment System Act, or any offence involving dishonesty directly related to Platform use;

6.2.5 **NLTA licence revocation** — where the Operator's operating licence is revoked and not reinstated within sixty (60) days;

6.2.6 **No-resale data breach** — sharing, selling, or misusing passenger or transaction data in violation of POPIA.

### 6.3 Procedure Before Permanent Ban

6.3.1 Prior to issuing a permanent ban, Tag-n-Ride shall:

(a) Provide the Operator / Driver with written notice of the alleged grounds;

(b) Allow the Operator / Driver five (5) business days to make written representations;

(c) Review the representations and issue a final written decision.

6.3.2 The right to make representations does not apply where immediate suspension is required to prevent ongoing harm or evidence destruction.

### 6.4 Consequences of Ban

Upon permanent deregistration:

- All QR Code Devices are immediately deactivated
- Digital Wallet balances (net of pending fraud investigations) are settled within 30 days
- NLTA associations and, where required, the FIC may be notified
- The individual or entity may not re-register under a different identity or entity
