## RECITALS

A. Tag-n-Ride operates a digital fare payment platform that enables cashless payments on minibus taxi routes;

B. The Association has brokered the commercial relationship between Tag-n-Ride and its member operators, including through onboarding, promotion, and facilitation of the Platform within the Association's membership;

C. In recognition of the Association's brokerage and facilitation role, the Parties have agreed that the Association shall receive a referral commission ("**Association Commission**") calculated as a defined percentage of Net Revenue generated by Association Members on the Platform;

D. The Parties wish to record the full terms of this revenue sharing arrangement.

**NOW THEREFORE** the Parties agree as follows:

---

## PART 1: DEFINITIONS

**"Active Member"** means an Association Member whose vehicles processed at least five (5) successful transactions during the relevant Settlement Period.

**"Association Commission"** means the referral commission payable to the Association as set out in Schedule A, calculated as a percentage of Net Revenue attributable to Association Members.

**"Association Members"** means taxi operators who are registered members of the Association and whose vehicles are activated on the Platform.

**"Audit Period"** means the twelve (12)-month period in respect of which an audit may be conducted under clause 5.

**"Chargeback"** means a transaction reversal initiated by a passenger or bank.

**"Gross Revenue"** means the total rand value of all completed fare transactions processed through the Platform by Association Members during a Settlement Period, before any deductions.

**"Net Revenue"** means Gross Revenue less: (i) Platform Fees; (ii) Chargebacks and refunds; (iii) Failed transaction reversals; (iv) Applicable bank charges directly related to transaction processing; and (v) Any government-mandated levies directly attributable to the transaction.

**"Platform Fee"** means Tag-n-Ride's fee deducted from each transaction as the consideration for providing and operating the Platform, at the rate specified in Schedule A.

**"Reconciliation Statement"** means the detailed monthly statement provided by Tag-n-Ride to the Association setting out the calculation of Gross Revenue, deductions, Net Revenue, and Association Commission for the relevant Settlement Period.

**"Settlement Period"** means each calendar month during the Term.

**"Tiered Commission"** means the volume-linked commission rate structure set out in Schedule A, where the Association Commission rate increases as transaction volumes grow.

## PART 2: ASSOCIATION COMMISSION — CALCULATION

### 2.1 Commission Entitlement

The Association shall be entitled to receive the Association Commission in respect of each Settlement Period during which at least the minimum number of Active Members specified in Schedule A are registered and active on the Platform.

### 2.2 Calculation Methodology

The Association Commission for each Settlement Period shall be calculated as follows:

```
Association Commission = Net Revenue (for the Period) × Commission Rate (%)
```

Where:
- **Net Revenue** is determined in accordance with the definition above and as detailed in the Reconciliation Statement
- **Commission Rate** is the applicable percentage from Schedule A (flat rate or tiered, as elected)

### 2.3 Tiered Commission Structure

If the Parties elect a tiered structure (as indicated in Schedule A), the following tiers apply:

| Monthly Net Revenue Band | Commission Rate |
|---|---|
| R0 — R[AMOUNT] | [X]% |
| R[AMOUNT] — R[AMOUNT] | [X]% |
| R[AMOUNT] — R[AMOUNT] | [X]% |
| Above R[AMOUNT] | [X]% |

*Note: Tiered rates apply only to the Net Revenue within each band, not to total Net Revenue.*

### 2.4 Non-Qualifying Transactions

The following transactions shall be excluded from the calculation of Net Revenue for Commission purposes:

2.4.1 Transactions subsequently reversed due to fraud attributable to Association Members or their Drivers;

2.4.2 Test transactions conducted during onboarding or system testing;

2.4.3 Transactions processed by Operators who have been suspended or deregistered from the Platform.

## PART 3: MONTHLY RECONCILIATION

### 3.1 Reconciliation Timeline

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tag-n-Ride prepares Reconciliation Statement | Within 10 business days after end of Settlement Period |
| 2 | Statement delivered to Association via email and portal | By the 12th business day |
| 3 | Association review period | 5 business days from receipt |
| 4 | Association raises written objections (if any) | Within review period |
| 5 | Parties resolve objections by mutual agreement | Within 10 business days of objection |
| 6 | Association Commission paid to Association | By the 20th business day after Settlement Period |

### 3.2 Reconciliation Statement Content

Each Reconciliation Statement shall include:

3.2.1 Total number of Active Members for the Settlement Period;

3.2.2 Total number of transactions processed by Association Members;

3.2.3 Gross Revenue broken down per Operator (anonymised if required by POPIA);

3.2.4 Itemised list of all deductions (Platform Fees, Chargebacks, refunds, etc.);

3.2.5 Net Revenue for the Settlement Period;

3.2.6 Applicable Commission Rate (including which tier, if tiered);

3.2.7 Association Commission amount due;

3.2.8 Year-to-date cumulative figures;

3.2.9 Running account of any disputed amounts.

### 3.3 Undisputed Amounts

Payment of the undisputed portion of the Association Commission shall not be withheld pending resolution of a dispute regarding any portion of the Reconciliation Statement.

## PART 5: AUDIT RIGHTS

### 5.1 Right to Audit

The Association shall have the right, exercisable once per Audit Period and on not less than fifteen (15) business days' written notice, to appoint an independent chartered accountant registered with SAICA to audit Tag-n-Ride's records relating to:

5.1.1 Transaction volumes and values attributable to Association Members;

5.1.2 The calculation of Gross Revenue, deductions, and Net Revenue;

5.1.3 The calculation and payment of the Association Commission.

### 5.2 Access to Records

Tag-n-Ride shall provide the appointed auditor with reasonable access to all relevant financial records, system logs, and reports during normal business hours, subject to appropriate confidentiality undertakings from the auditor.

### 5.3 Audit Costs

5.3.1 The Association shall bear the cost of any audit it commissions.

5.3.2 If the audit reveals that Tag-n-Ride has underpaid the Association Commission by more than five percent (5%) in the Audit Period, Tag-n-Ride shall:

(a) Pay the shortfall amount together with interest at the prime lending rate plus two percent (2%) from the date each underpayment was due; and

(b) Reimburse the Association for the reasonable cost of the audit.

### 5.4 Record Retention

Tag-n-Ride shall retain all records relevant to the calculation of the Association Commission for a minimum of five (5) years, or such longer period as may be required by law.

---

## PART 6: ASSOCIATION OBLIGATIONS

In consideration of receiving the Association Commission, the Association undertakes to:

6.1 Actively promote the Platform to all eligible members and encourage adoption;

6.2 Maintain a minimum number of Active Members as set out in Schedule A (failure to maintain this minimum may result in Commission rate adjustment as set out in Schedule A);

6.3 Cooperate with Tag-n-Ride's onboarding process for new member operators;

6.4 Notify Tag-n-Ride of any member's operating licence changes within five (5) business days;

6.5 Not direct Association Members to use a competing digital payment platform during any Exclusivity Period agreed between the Parties;

6.6 Not misrepresent the Platform or its fee structure to Association Members;

6.7 Provide Tag-n-Ride with updated Association membership lists on a quarterly basis.

---

## PART 7: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 7.1 Negotiation

Any Dispute shall first be referred to senior representatives of both Parties for good faith resolution within fifteen (15) business days.

### 7.2 Mediation

If unresolved, the Dispute shall be referred to mediation before a mutually agreed mediator or, failing agreement, a mediator appointed by AFSA.

### 7.3 Arbitration

If mediation fails within thirty (30) days, the Dispute shall be referred to binding arbitration under AFSA Rules in Johannesburg.

### 7.4 Undisputed Payments

No dispute shall entitle either Party to withhold payment of any undisputed amounts.
