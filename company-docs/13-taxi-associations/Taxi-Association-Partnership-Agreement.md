## RECITALS

**WHEREAS:**

A. Tag-n-Ride is a South African financial technology company that has developed and operates a digital payment platform (the "**Platform**") enabling cashless fare payments on public transport, including minibus taxis, through QR code-based technology and mobile wallet infrastructure;

B. The Association is a registered taxi association operating within the public road transport sector in accordance with the National Land Transport Act 5 of 2009 ("**NLTA**") and is duly authorised to represent and bind its member operators and drivers in respect of matters within the scope of this Agreement;

C. The Parties wish to enter into a formal partnership to deploy the Tag-n-Ride digital payment platform across the Association's registered fleet and routes, to the mutual benefit of the Association, its members, and the travelling public;

D. The Parties acknowledge the importance of transforming the South African public transport sector through digital financial inclusion, in alignment with government policy objectives including the National Development Plan and the Financial Sector Regulation Act 9 of 2017 objectives;

E. The Parties have agreed to collaborate on the terms and conditions set out herein.

## PART 1: DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

### 1.1 Definitions

In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires:

**"Active Vehicle"** means any vehicle registered on the Platform that has processed at least one (1) successful transaction in the immediately preceding calendar month.

**"Applicable Laws"** means all statutes, regulations, bylaws, codes, ordinances, and other legal instruments of the Republic of South Africa applicable to the subject matter of this Agreement, including but not limited to the NLTA, POPIA, FICA, and the National Payment System Act 78 of 1998.

**"Association Members"** means taxi operators and drivers who are registered members of the Association and whose vehicles or services are covered by the Association's operating licences.

**"Confidential Information"** means all non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other in connection with this Agreement, whether in written, electronic, oral, or any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

**"CPA"** means the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008.

**"Digital Wallet"** means the electronic wallet maintained within the Platform for each registered operator, into which fare receipts are settled after deduction of the Platform Fee.

**"Dispute"** means any disagreement, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity, breach, or termination.

**"Driver"** means a person who operates a taxi vehicle that is registered on the Platform as a member of the Association.

**"FICA"** means the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001, as amended.

**"Gross Revenue"** means the total value of all completed fare transactions processed through the Platform by Association Members in a given settlement period, before deduction of any fees, refunds, or chargebacks.

**"Initial Term"** means the period of twenty-four (24) months commencing on the Effective Date.

**"Net Revenue"** means Gross Revenue less refunds, chargebacks, failed transaction reversals, and any regulatory levies directly attributable to a specific transaction.

**"NLTA"** means the National Land Transport Act 5 of 2009, as amended, and includes all regulations, notices, and guidelines issued thereunder.

**"NLTA Operating Licence"** means the operating licence issued to an Association Member under section 47 of the NLTA authorising the operation of a specified public transport service on defined routes.

**"Operator"** means a taxi owner who is an Association Member and who registers one or more vehicles on the Platform.

**"Platform"** means the Tag-n-Ride digital payment technology platform, including all software, applications, QR code hardware, payment processing infrastructure, and associated services.

**"Platform Fee"** means the percentage-based fee deducted by Tag-n-Ride from Gross Revenue for each transaction, as set out in Schedule A.

**"POPIA"** means the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013.

**"QR Code Device"** means the physical or digital QR code assigned to a specific vehicle for the purpose of enabling passenger payments through the Platform.

**"Renewal Term"** means any further period of twelve (12) months for which this Agreement is renewed pursuant to clause 5.2.

**"Settlement Period"** means each calendar month during the Term.

**"Territory"** means the geographic areas within which the Association's members are authorised to operate under their NLTA Operating Licences.

## PART 2: SCOPE OF PARTNERSHIP

### 2.1 Nature of Partnership

The Parties hereby agree to enter into a non-exclusive commercial partnership (unless exclusivity is elected under clause 9) for the deployment of the Platform across the Association's registered fleet and routes. This Agreement does not create a legal partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the Parties.

### 2.2 Tag-n-Ride Obligations

Tag-n-Ride undertakes to:

2.2.1 Provide, maintain, and support the Platform in a functional state throughout the Term;

2.2.2 Issue QR Code Devices to all registered Association Member vehicles upon successful KYC verification of the relevant Operator, at no upfront cost to the Association (unless otherwise agreed in Schedule A);

2.2.3 Provide onboarding training to Association Member Operators and Drivers as set out in Schedule B;

2.2.4 Maintain a dedicated Association support line and relationship manager assigned to the Association;

2.2.5 Process all fare transactions in accordance with the National Payment System Act 78 of 1998 and applicable payment regulations;

2.2.6 Provide monthly settlement reports to the Association detailing Gross Revenue, Platform Fees, Net Revenue, and distributions per Operator;

2.2.7 Maintain all required regulatory licences and authorisations to operate the Platform as a payment service in South Africa;

2.2.8 Implement and maintain reasonable cybersecurity and data protection measures in compliance with POPIA and applicable standards;

2.2.9 Provide the Association with a read-only dashboard to monitor aggregated transaction data for their membership.

### 2.3 Association Obligations

The Association undertakes to:

2.3.1 Actively promote and facilitate the adoption of the Platform among its members;

2.3.2 Require all new Operators seeking registration on the Platform to comply with the onboarding requirements set out in Schedule C;

2.3.3 Ensure that its members comply with the Tag-n-Ride Taxi Industry Code of Conduct;

2.3.4 Assist Tag-n-Ride in verifying the NLTA Operating Licence status of Operator applicants;

2.3.5 Notify Tag-n-Ride within five (5) business days of any member's operating licence being suspended, revoked, or lapsing;

2.3.6 Cooperate with Tag-n-Ride in any regulatory audit or compliance inspection relating to the Platform's operation within the Association's routes;

2.3.7 Designate a named liaison officer for day-to-day coordination with Tag-n-Ride;

2.3.8 Not enter into any agreement with a competing digital fare payment platform during an Exclusivity Period (if applicable under clause 9).

## PART 3: DIGITAL PAYMENT INTEGRATION

### 3.1 Technical Integration

3.1.1 Tag-n-Ride shall deploy its Platform across the Association's member fleet using a phased rollout plan agreed in writing by the Parties prior to the Effective Date ("**Rollout Plan**").

3.1.2 Integration shall include issuance of QR Code Devices, Driver app installation support, and Operator wallet configuration.

3.1.3 The Association shall make reasonable efforts to facilitate vehicle access for QR Code Device installation during the rollout phase.

### 3.2 Payment Processing

3.2.1 All fare payments processed through the Platform shall be subject to the National Payment System Act 78 of 1998 and any regulations issued by the South African Reserve Bank ("**SARB**") thereunder.

3.2.2 Passenger funds collected through the Platform shall be held in a designated trust or payment account as required by applicable law before settlement to Operators.

3.2.3 Settlement to Operator Digital Wallets shall occur within the timeframes specified in Schedule A, subject to successful transaction verification.

### 3.3 QR Code Compliance

3.3.1 Each QR Code Device is linked to a specific vehicle and its registered Operator. Transfer of QR Code Devices between vehicles without Tag-n-Ride's written authorisation is prohibited.

3.3.2 The Association shall assist in managing QR code integrity within its membership and shall report suspected misuse to Tag-n-Ride immediately.

---

## PART 4: REVENUE SHARE AND PLATFORM FEE STRUCTURE

### 4.1 Platform Fee

4.1.1 Tag-n-Ride shall deduct a Platform Fee from all transactions processed through the Platform. The Platform Fee rate applicable to Association Members is set out in **Schedule A** to this Agreement.

4.1.2 The Platform Fee is inclusive of all technology, payment processing, settlement, and support costs borne by Tag-n-Ride.

4.1.3 Tag-n-Ride reserves the right to revise the Platform Fee upon sixty (60) days' written notice to the Association. The Association may object within thirty (30) days of notice, and the Parties shall negotiate in good faith. If no agreement is reached within the thirty (30) day objection period, either Party may terminate this Agreement on sixty (60) days' notice.

### 4.2 Association Commission

4.2.1 Tag-n-Ride shall pay the Association a referral commission ("**Association Commission**") as set out in Schedule A, calculated as a percentage of Net Revenue generated by Association Members during each Settlement Period.

4.2.2 The Association Commission shall be paid into the Association's designated bank account within fifteen (15) business days after the end of each Settlement Period, together with a detailed reconciliation statement.

4.2.3 The Association Commission is subject to deduction of applicable VAT where the Association is a VAT vendor registered under the Value-Added Tax Act 89 of 1991.

### 4.3 Reconciliation and Audit

4.3.1 Tag-n-Ride shall provide the Association with a monthly reconciliation statement within ten (10) business days after the end of each Settlement Period.

4.3.2 The Association shall have the right, on not less than fifteen (15) business days' written notice and not more than once per calendar year, to appoint an independent auditor to audit the records relating to transactions processed under this Agreement. The cost of such audit shall be borne by the Association unless the audit reveals a discrepancy of more than five percent (5%) in the Association's favour, in which case Tag-n-Ride shall bear the audit costs.

## PART 5: TERM AND RENEWAL

### 5.1 Initial Term

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for the Initial Term of twenty-four (24) months, unless earlier terminated in accordance with Part 8.

### 5.2 Renewal

5.2.1 Upon expiry of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive Renewal Terms of twelve (12) months each, unless either Party gives written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days before the expiry of the then-current term.

5.2.2 During any Renewal Term, the terms of this Agreement (including Schedule A) shall apply unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

### 5.3 Review

The Parties shall conduct a formal review of this Agreement at the end of the first twelve (12) months of the Initial Term to assess performance against targets set out in Schedule B and to consider any amendments.

## PART 6: NLTA COMPLIANCE

### 6.1 Regulatory Compliance

6.1.1 The Parties acknowledge that the Association and its members operate within a regulated transport environment under the NLTA and associated regulations, including the National Land Transport Regulations, 2009, and relevant provincial legislation.

6.1.2 Tag-n-Ride shall not require any Association Member to operate in a manner inconsistent with the conditions of their NLTA Operating Licence.

6.1.3 The Association warrants that it has the necessary authority to enter into this Agreement and to bind its members in respect of the obligations contemplated herein.

### 6.2 Operating Licence Verification

6.2.1 Prior to activating any vehicle on the Platform, Tag-n-Ride shall verify (with the Association's assistance) that the vehicle is covered by a valid NLTA Operating Licence.

6.2.2 Tag-n-Ride shall maintain a register of all activated vehicles and their corresponding NLTA Operating Licence references.

### 6.3 Regulatory Changes

Should any change in the NLTA or transport regulations materially affect the obligations or economics of this Agreement, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to amend this Agreement accordingly within sixty (60) days of the regulatory change taking effect.

---

## PART 7: DATA OWNERSHIP AND POPIA COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 Data Ownership

7.1.1 Transaction data generated through the Platform ("**Transaction Data**") shall be owned jointly by the Parties subject to the limitations set out herein.

7.1.2 Tag-n-Ride shall have the right to use Transaction Data (in anonymised and aggregated form) for product development, analytics, and reporting purposes.

7.1.3 The Association shall have the right to access anonymised aggregated Transaction Data relating to its members via the Association dashboard.

7.1.4 Neither Party shall sell, transfer, or otherwise commercially exploit raw personal Transaction Data of individual passengers or Operators to third parties without the explicit consent of the relevant data subjects.

### 7.2 Personal Information

7.2.1 Both Parties shall comply with POPIA in respect of all personal information processed in connection with this Agreement.

7.2.2 Tag-n-Ride acts as the responsible party (as defined in POPIA) in respect of personal information collected through the Platform from passengers, Operators, and Drivers.

7.2.3 The Association shall maintain its own privacy compliance obligations in respect of personal information it holds about its members.

7.2.4 In the event of a personal information security compromise relating to data processed under this Agreement, the Party becoming aware of the compromise shall notify the other Party and the Information Regulator (as required under section 22 of POPIA) without undue delay.

### 7.3 Data Security

Tag-n-Ride shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information processed through the Platform, including encryption, access controls, and regular security assessments.

## PART 8: TERMINATION

### 8.1 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement on sixty (60) days' written notice to the other Party after the expiry of the first twelve (12) months of the Initial Term.

### 8.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the other Party if:

8.2.1 The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days of written notice requiring it to do so;

8.2.2 The other Party becomes insolvent, is placed in liquidation or business rescue, makes an arrangement with its creditors, or has a judgment granted against it that remains unsatisfied for more than thirty (30) days;

8.2.3 The other Party engages in fraudulent, dishonest, or corrupt conduct in connection with this Agreement;

8.2.4 A regulatory authority suspends or revokes a material licence or authorisation of the other Party that is necessary for the performance of this Agreement.

### 8.3 Consequences of Termination

8.3.1 Upon termination, Tag-n-Ride shall deactivate all QR Code Devices associated with the Association's members.

8.3.2 Tag-n-Ride shall process final settlement of all outstanding balances in Operator Digital Wallets within thirty (30) days of the termination date.

8.3.3 Each Party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information upon request.

8.3.4 Termination shall not affect any accrued rights, obligations, or liabilities of either Party.

### 8.4 Survival

Clauses 4.3, 7, 10, 11, and 12 shall survive the termination or expiry of this Agreement.

## PART 9: EXCLUSIVITY

### 9.1 Exclusivity Election

The Parties may elect for an exclusivity arrangement on mutually agreed terms by executing Schedule D to this Agreement. Absent an executed Schedule D, this Agreement is non-exclusive.

### 9.2 Non-Exclusive Default

In the absence of an exclusivity arrangement:

9.2.1 Tag-n-Ride may partner with other taxi associations, operators, or transport providers;

9.2.2 The Association may engage other payment technology providers, provided it does not prejudice its obligations under this Agreement.

## PART 10: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 10.1 Good Faith Negotiation

If a Dispute arises, the Parties shall first attempt to resolve it through good faith negotiation between their respective senior representatives within fifteen (15) business days of written notice of the Dispute.

### 10.2 Industry Body Mediation

10.2.1 If the Dispute is not resolved under clause 10.1, either Party may refer the Dispute to mediation facilitated by a mutually agreed-upon representative of a relevant taxi industry body (such as SANTACO — the South African National Taxi Council) or, if the Parties cannot agree, by a mediator appointed by the Arbitration Foundation of Southern Africa ("**AFSA**").

10.2.2 The costs of mediation shall be shared equally between the Parties.

### 10.3 Arbitration

10.3.1 If mediation under clause 10.2 fails to resolve the Dispute within thirty (30) days of the commencement of mediation, either Party may refer the Dispute to binding arbitration under the AFSA Arbitration Rules in force at the time.

10.3.2 The seat of arbitration shall be Johannesburg, South Africa.

10.3.3 The arbitration shall be conducted in English.

10.3.4 The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding.

### 10.4 Urgent Relief

Nothing in this clause 10 shall prevent either Party from seeking urgent or interim relief from a court of competent jurisdiction where necessary to protect its rights.
