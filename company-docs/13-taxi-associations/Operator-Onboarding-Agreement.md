## RECITALS

## PART 1: DEFINITIONS

In this Agreement:

**"Applicable Laws"** means all South African legislation applicable to the subject matter of this Agreement, including the National Land Transport Act 5 of 2009 ("**NLTA**"), the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001 ("**FICA**"), the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("**POPIA**"), the National Payment System Act 78 of 1998, the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 ("**CPA**"), and the Value-Added Tax Act 89 of 1991.

**"Chargeback"** means a transaction reversal initiated by a passenger or financial institution in respect of a disputed or fraudulent transaction.

**"Digital Wallet"** means the electronic wallet maintained on the Platform in the Operator's name, into which settled fare proceeds are credited after deduction of the Platform Fee.

**"Driver"** means any person authorised by the Operator to operate a Registered Vehicle and who is registered on the Platform as a Driver for that vehicle.

**"FICA Due Diligence"** means the know-your-customer and anti-money laundering verification process prescribed by FICA and the Financial Intelligence Centre's guidance notes.

**"KYC"** means Know Your Customer — the process of verifying the identity, address, and legitimacy of a person or entity in accordance with FICA and Tag-n-Ride's internal policies.

**"Platform Fee"** means the fee deducted by Tag-n-Ride from gross transaction proceeds, as specified in Schedule A.

**"QR Code Device"** means the physical or digital QR code assigned to each Registered Vehicle for passenger payments.

**"Registered Vehicle"** means each vehicle registered by the Operator on the Platform under this Agreement, as listed in Schedule B.

**"Settlement"** means the transfer of Net Revenue to the Operator's Digital Wallet or linked bank account after deduction of the Platform Fee, Chargebacks, and any other authorised deductions.

**"Term"** means the period from the Platform Activation Date until termination of this Agreement.

## PART 2: OPERATOR ELIGIBILITY AND KYC

### 2.1 Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for onboarding and Platform access, the Operator must:

2.1.1 Hold a valid NLTA Operating Licence covering each vehicle to be registered;

2.1.2 Be a registered member in good standing of a recognised taxi association;

2.1.3 Pass Tag-n-Ride's KYC and FICA due diligence process to the satisfaction of Tag-n-Ride;

2.1.4 Hold a valid South African bank account in the Operator's name or entity name;

2.1.5 Not be under business rescue, liquidation, or subject to any court order that would prevent performance under this Agreement;

2.1.6 Hold a valid South African Income Tax registration number.

### 2.2 KYC Documentation

The Operator must submit the following documents before activation:

| Document | Requirement |
|---|---|
| South African ID document | Certified copy (not older than 3 months) |
| NLTA Operating Licence/s | Certified copy for each vehicle |
| Vehicle registration certificate/s | Certified copy for each Registered Vehicle |
| Proof of residential or business address | Not older than 3 months |
| Bank account confirmation letter | From a registered South African bank |
| Tax clearance pin / TCS status | Current and valid |
| Proof of taxi association membership | Letter from association on letterhead |
| Entity documents (if applicable) | CIPC certificate, MOI, resolution to sign |

### 2.3 FICA Obligations

2.3.1 The Operator acknowledges that Tag-n-Ride is required to conduct customer due diligence in accordance with FICA and the regulations and guidance notes issued thereunder.

2.3.2 The Operator consents to Tag-n-Ride conducting identity verification, adverse media checks, PEP (politically exposed person) screening, and sanctions screening as part of the FICA process.

2.3.3 The Operator must promptly notify Tag-n-Ride of any material change to the information provided during KYC, including changes to ownership, banking details, operating licence status, or home address.

2.3.4 Tag-n-Ride reserves the right to suspend or terminate the Operator's account where FICA compliance cannot be maintained or where suspicious transaction activity is detected.

2.3.5 Tag-n-Ride is required by law to report suspicious transactions and activities to the Financial Intelligence Centre ("**FIC**") in terms of section 29 of FICA. The Operator shall not be notified of any such report.

### 2.4 Ongoing Monitoring

Tag-n-Ride shall conduct periodic re-verification of KYC information as required by FICA, and the Operator agrees to cooperate with all such re-verification requests within fourteen (14) days.

## PART 3: QR CODE ISSUANCE

### 3.1 Issuance

3.1.1 Upon successful completion of the KYC process and execution of this Agreement, Tag-n-Ride shall issue a unique QR Code Device for each Registered Vehicle listed in Schedule B.

3.1.2 Each QR Code Device is uniquely linked to the relevant Registered Vehicle and may only be used in that vehicle.

### 3.2 Operator Obligations Regarding QR Codes

The Operator undertakes to:

3.2.1 Display the QR Code Device in a clearly visible location within each Registered Vehicle as specified in Tag-n-Ride's installation guide;

3.2.2 Keep the QR Code Device secure and in good working condition;

3.2.3 Immediately report any lost, damaged, or stolen QR Code Device to Tag-n-Ride;

3.2.4 Not duplicate, modify, or transfer a QR Code Device to any other vehicle or person without Tag-n-Ride's prior written consent;

3.2.5 Not use a QR Code Device for any vehicle that is not covered by a valid NLTA Operating Licence.

### 3.3 Replacement

Tag-n-Ride shall replace damaged or malfunctioning QR Code Devices at no charge for the first instance per vehicle per year. Subsequent replacements may be charged at the rate set out in Schedule A.

## PART 5: PLATFORM RULES

### 5.1 Permitted Use

The Operator may use the Platform solely for:

5.1.1 Receiving digital fare payments from passengers on NLTA-authorised routes;

5.1.2 Managing Driver accounts for Registered Vehicles;

5.1.3 Accessing transaction reports and wallet balances.

### 5.2 Prohibited Conduct

The Operator must not:

5.2.1 Use the Platform for any purpose other than legitimate taxi fare collection;

5.2.2 Attempt to process fraudulent, fictitious, or inflated transactions;

5.2.3 Split transactions to avoid monitoring thresholds in contravention of FICA;

5.2.4 Allow a third party (not a registered Driver for the specific vehicle) to use a QR Code Device;

5.2.5 Coerce or pressure passengers to pay via the Platform or to refrain from using it;

5.2.6 Share login credentials with unauthorised persons;

5.2.7 Engage in any conduct that constitutes money laundering, fraud, or corruption in terms of South African law.

### 5.3 Driver Management

5.3.1 The Operator is responsible for registering and managing Drivers for each Registered Vehicle on the Platform.

5.3.2 The Operator warrants that each registered Driver holds a valid professional driving permit ("**PDP**") and is legally entitled to drive the relevant Registered Vehicle as a public transport operator.

5.3.3 The Operator shall immediately deregister any Driver from the Platform upon termination of the Driver's employment or engagement.

## PART 6: PLATFORM FEE AND SETTLEMENT

### 6.1 Platform Fee

6.1.1 Tag-n-Ride shall deduct the Platform Fee from each transaction as set out in Schedule A.

6.1.2 The Platform Fee is all-inclusive and covers payment processing, platform maintenance, settlement, and support.

### 6.2 Settlement

6.2.1 Settled amounts shall be credited to the Operator's Digital Wallet on a [daily / weekly / monthly] basis as specified in Schedule A.

6.2.2 The Operator shall receive a transaction statement accessible via the Platform app or dashboard.

### 6.3 Deductions

In addition to the Platform Fee, Tag-n-Ride may deduct from the Digital Wallet:
- Chargebacks attributable to fraudulent or disputed transactions processed through the Operator's QR Codes
- Penalties for material Platform rule violations (as set out in Schedule A)
- Any outstanding amounts owed by the Operator to Tag-n-Ride

## PART 7: LIABILITY

### 7.1 Tag-n-Ride's Liability

7.1.1 Tag-n-Ride shall not be liable for any loss or damage suffered by the Operator arising from:

(a) System downtime or maintenance interruptions communicated in advance;

(b) Failure of third-party payment infrastructure (banks, PASA-linked systems);

(c) Acts or omissions of Drivers or passengers;

(d) Force majeure events.

7.1.2 Tag-n-Ride's total aggregate liability to the Operator under this Agreement shall not exceed the total Platform Fees paid by the Operator in the three (3) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

### 7.2 Operator's Liability

7.2.1 The Operator shall be liable for all losses arising from fraudulent use of the Operator's QR Code Devices or Platform account, where such fraud results from the Operator's negligence or failure to comply with Platform rules.

7.2.2 The Operator shall indemnify Tag-n-Ride against any claim, loss, or liability arising from the Operator's violation of Applicable Laws, Platform rules, or this Agreement.

### 7.3 No Liability for Passenger Disputes

Tag-n-Ride is not a party to the transport services agreement between the Operator and passengers. Tag-n-Ride is a payment facilitator and shall not be liable for any service-related disputes between operators and passengers.

## PART 9: TERMINATION

### 9.1 Termination by Operator

The Operator may terminate this Agreement on thirty (30) days' written notice to Tag-n-Ride, provided all outstanding amounts in the Digital Wallet have been withdrawn and no pending investigations are underway.

### 9.2 Termination by Tag-n-Ride

Tag-n-Ride may terminate this Agreement immediately on written notice if:

9.2.1 The Operator's NLTA Operating Licence is suspended, revoked, or lapses;

9.2.2 The Operator fails KYC or FICA re-verification;

9.2.3 The Operator is found to have committed fraud, money laundering, or material Platform rule violations;

9.2.4 The Operator's taxi association membership is terminated;

9.2.5 The Operator becomes insolvent or is placed in liquidation.

### 9.3 Consequences

Upon termination:
- QR Code Devices shall be immediately deactivated
- Outstanding Digital Wallet balances shall be settled within thirty (30) days (subject to fraud or compliance holds)
- QR Code Devices (if physical) must be returned to Tag-n-Ride within fourteen (14) days
