### 1.2 Why a Legal Hold is Necessary

In South African litigation and arbitration proceedings, all parties are obligated to preserve documents, data, and information that are or may be relevant to the proceedings. Destruction, alteration, or loss of relevant information after a legal hold has been issued can constitute:

1.2.1 **Spoliation of evidence** — which may result in adverse inferences being drawn against Tag-n-Ride by a court or tribunal;

1.2.2 **Contempt of court** — where a preservation order has been issued;

1.2.3 **Disciplinary action** against employees who deliberately destroy or withhold relevant documents;

1.2.4 **Criminal liability** under the Prevention of Organised Crime Act 121 of 1998 or the Prevention and Combating of Corrupt Activities Act 12 of 2004, where destruction is intended to obstruct justice.

## PART 3: EMPLOYEE OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Immediate Actions Required

Each recipient of this Notice must, immediately upon receipt:

3.1.1 **Stop all destruction or deletion** of documents, emails, files, records, or data that may relate to the matter described in Part 1 — even if such deletion was previously scheduled as part of a routine document retention or destruction programme;

3.1.2 **Locate and identify** all documents (physical and electronic) within your possession, custody, or control that fall within the scope described in Part 2;

3.1.3 **Do not alter** any document, metadata, or record in any way. This includes editing, annotating, overwriting, or otherwise modifying documents that may be relevant;

3.1.4 **Notify IT** immediately if you are aware of any system that may be scheduled to overwrite, delete, or archive data that falls within the scope of this hold;

3.1.5 **Report** to [LEGAL COUNSEL / COMPLIANCE OFFICER NAME] any document or information you have already destroyed or cannot locate, as soon as you become aware.

### 3.2 Ongoing Obligations

During the period this Legal Hold Notice remains in force:

3.2.1 Continue to preserve all documents within scope as they are created, received, or modified;

3.2.2 Do not discuss the contents of documents under hold with any person outside the preserved scope without prior authorisation from the supervising attorney;

3.2.3 Respond promptly to all requests from legal counsel or the compliance team for documents and information;

3.2.4 Inform the compliance officer if you leave the employ of Tag-n-Ride before this hold is lifted, and hand over all documents under hold before departure.

### 3.3 Personal Devices

If any relevant documents exist on your personal devices (personal email, personal phone, personal laptop), you are required to:

3.3.1 Preserve those documents;

3.3.2 Notify the compliance officer immediately;

3.3.3 Cooperate with any request to provide copies of such documents.

## PART 4: IT OBLIGATIONS

The IT team is required to immediately implement the following technical preservation measures:

### 4.1 Email Preservation

4.1.1 Place a litigation hold on the email accounts of all persons listed in the Recipients table;

4.1.2 Disable automatic email deletion, archiving, or purging for affected accounts;

4.1.3 Create and preserve an email archive for the relevant date range.

### 4.2 System Data Preservation

4.2.1 Suspend all scheduled data deletion, purging, log rotation, or database archiving processes that may affect data within the scope of this hold;

4.2.2 Create a full backup or forensic image of the relevant Platform databases, transaction logs, and server logs for the relevant date range;

4.2.3 Disable auto-delete functions on relevant data stores.

### 4.3 Physical Storage

4.3.1 Secure and lock any physical storage locations containing documents within the scope of this hold;

4.3.2 Maintain a chain of custody log for any physical documents identified.

### 4.4 Cloud Systems

4.4.1 Preserve data held in cloud-based systems (e.g., Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, AWS S3, cloud payment processors) relevant to the scope of this hold;

4.4.2 Notify cloud service providers of the hold if necessary to prevent automatic deletion.

### 4.5 IT Confirmation

The IT Lead must provide written confirmation to the Compliance Officer within three (3) Business Days of receipt of this Notice, confirming which preservation measures have been implemented.

## PART 5: CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE

### 5.1 Disciplinary Consequences

Any employee who knowingly destroys, deletes, alters, or withholds documents after receiving this Notice may face:

5.1.1 Formal disciplinary proceedings under Tag-n-Ride's disciplinary code, up to and including dismissal for gross misconduct;

5.1.2 Personal civil liability for the harm caused to Tag-n-Ride by the destruction of evidence.

### 5.2 Legal Consequences

Under South African law:

5.2.1 Deliberate destruction of evidence may constitute:

(a) Tampering with evidence — an offence under the Criminal Procedure Act 51 of 1977;

(b) Obstruction of justice under common law;

(c) Corrupt activity under the Prevention and Combating of Corrupt Activities Act 12 of 2004, where done for corrupt purposes.

5.2.2 Tag-n-Ride may suffer adverse court rulings if it cannot produce relevant documents — including negative inferences, cost orders, or dismissal of claims.

## PART 6: DURATION AND LIFTING OF HOLD

### 6.1 Duration

This Legal Hold Notice shall remain in force until formally lifted by a written release notice issued by Tag-n-Ride's CEO or supervising legal counsel.

### 6.2 Lifting the Hold

The hold will be lifted when:

6.2.1 The litigation, arbitration, or investigation has been fully resolved (including all appeals and enforcement proceedings);

6.2.2 All limitation periods for further claims have expired;

6.2.3 Legal counsel confirms in writing that preservation is no longer required.

### 6.3 Post-Hold

Upon lifting of the hold, normal document retention and destruction procedures may resume in respect of documents covered by the hold (subject to any court or regulatory order requiring further retention).
